How to Send Money to Philippines — Steps

  1. Get recipient details: full name, Philippine bank account or GCash/PayMaya mobile number, mother's maiden name (required by some banks), city for cash pickup.
  2. Open Saudi remittance app (STC Pay, Al Rajhi, Tahweel branch).
  3. Choose Philippines → payout method (bank/GCash/PayMaya/cash pickup).
  4. Enter SAR amount — confirm PHP amount.
  5. Authorize with biometric or OTP.
  6. Share transfer reference number with family for tracking.

GCash, PayMaya, Maya — Mobile Wallet Tips

The Philippines' top mobile wallets — GCash (now owned by Globe Fintech Innovations), PayMaya/Maya, and Coins.ph — are widely supported by Saudi remittance providers. GCash transfers are typically instant and have lower fees than bank deposits. They have replaced cash pickup as the most popular OFW receiving method.

Cash Pickup Options in the Philippines

If your family prefers cash, these networks have nationwide branches:

  • M.Lhuillier — 2,500+ branches, partner with most Saudi remittance services
  • Cebuana Lhuillier — 2,500+ branches, especially strong in rural areas
  • Palawan Pawnshop — 3,500+ branches, popular in Visayas and Mindanao
  • BDO Remit — major bank network for cash pickup or account credit
  • LBC Express — combines remittance with parcel delivery

Required Documents (First-Time Senders)

  • Valid Iqama or Saudi national ID.
  • Saudi mobile number registered to your Iqama.
  • For large transfers (above SAR 30,000/month) — salary slip or employment letter.
  • Recipient's full name (as in their official ID).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best way to send money from Saudi Arabia to Philippines in 2026?+
For monthly OFW allotments to family, STC Pay or Al Rajhi Tahweel direct to GCash gives the best combined rate + fee + speed (instant delivery, low fee). For larger amounts, Wise offers the best mid-market rate. For cash pickup at M.Lhuillier/Cebuana, Western Union or MoneyGram.
What is the current SAR to PHP exchange rate?+
As of May 2026, 1 SAR is approximately 14.60–14.85 PHP depending on provider. Mid-market rate is around 14.85 PHP. Most providers add a 0.5–1.5% spread.
Can I send money to GCash, PayMaya, or Coins.ph?+
Yes. All major Saudi remittance providers (STC Pay, Al Rajhi, Enjaz, Tahweel) support direct transfer to GCash, PayMaya/Maya, and Coins.ph mobile wallets. Mobile wallet transfers are usually instant and cheaper than bank deposits.
Where can my family receive cash in the Philippines?+
Major cash-pickup networks include M.Lhuillier (2,500+ branches), Cebuana Lhuillier (2,500+ branches), Palawan Pawnshop (3,500+ branches), BDO Remit, and LBC Express. Saudi providers partner with most of these — choose the one closest to your family.
Are there any fees for receiving money in the Philippines?+
Bank deposits and GCash/PayMaya transfers are typically fee-free for the recipient. Cash pickup may have a small Philippine-side handling fee (~1%) but most Saudi providers include this in the sender's fee.
