Saudi Gold Prices 2026 — What Moves the Daily Rate

Saudi Arabia is the GCC's largest gold market by physical demand. Daily gold prices on this page reflect the wholesale spot price converted to SAR, before retail margins and making charges. Whether you're buying 22-karat jewellery in Taibah or Batha souks, valuing inherited gold for Zakat, or simply tracking the market, understanding the difference between the international spot price and what you pay at a Saudi shop is the difference between a fair deal and a 15% overpay. The live prices above refresh hourly; the guide below explains the maths.

What determines the daily gold price in Saudi Arabia?

The global spot price of gold (set in London) is the starting point. It's quoted in USD per troy ounce (31.1035 grams). To get the SAR per gram price you see in Saudi shops:

SAR/gram = (USD/oz × 3.75) ÷ 31.1035

Worked example: If London spot is USD 2,420/oz, then SAR/gram (24K) = (2,420 × 3.75) ÷ 31.1035 = SAR 291.80/gram for pure 24K gold. For 22K (91.6% purity) the price is 291.80 × 0.9166 = SAR 267.45/gram. For 21K it's 291.80 × 0.875 = SAR 255.33/gram. For 18K it's 291.80 × 0.750 = SAR 218.85/gram. These are wholesale prices — shops add making fee and retail margin on top.

Making charges — the part most buyers underestimate

In Saudi Arabia, jewellery is sold at gold weight × current price + a making charge per gram. Making charges range from SAR 5 per gram (mass-market chains like Damas, L'azurde) to SAR 50+ per gram (designer pieces and pure-handmade Khaleeji styles). For an investment piece — bullion, plain bangles, sovereign coins — the making charge is minimal (SAR 3–10/g). For an elaborate wedding set, making can be SAR 30–80/g and easily double the gold-content price.

The buy-back catch. When you sell back the same jewellery, the shop pays you only the gold-content value at that day's spot — the making charge is non-refundable. So a SAR 10,000 wedding bangle bought today (60g × SAR 110/g content + SAR 60/g making = SAR 6,600 + SAR 3,600) might fetch only SAR 6,800 when sold next year, even if gold prices didn't drop.

Buying gold in Saudi Arabia — practical tips

  • Always check the karat stamp. 24K = "999" or "24K"; 22K = "916"; 21K = "875"; 18K = "750". The stamp is required by Saudi law on every piece above 1g.
  • Weigh in front of you. Reputable shops weigh and show you the SAMA-calibrated scale reading before pricing.
  • Get an itemised receipt. The receipt must show gold weight in grams, karat, gold rate per gram on that day, and making charge separately.
  • Compare three shops on the same day. Gold-content price is the same everywhere, but making charges can vary 50% even within Batha Riyadh. Walk three shops before deciding.
  • Pay by mada or cash with receipt. Credit card surcharges of 2–3% are common; mada has no surcharge. Cash with no receipt skips VAT but voids your buy-back rights.

Is there VAT on gold in Saudi Arabia?

Investment-grade gold (bullion of 24K / 999 purity in bar or coin form, weighing 1 gram or more) is zero-rated for VAT under ZATCA rules. Jewellery (22K, 21K, 18K, or any worked gold) IS subject to 15% VAT — applied to the full price including making. So that SAR 10,000 wedding set actually costs SAR 11,500 at checkout. Frequently asked questions

Which karat is best for everyday wear in Saudi Arabia?

21K is the GCC standard for everyday jewellery — it's harder than 22K (more durable for daily wear) and cheaper, while still being recognisably "real gold" by Khaleeji standards. 22K is the wedding/festive standard. 24K is too soft for jewellery and used mostly for bullion or coins. 18K is European-style and less popular in Saudi markets.

Can I take gold out of Saudi Arabia when I leave?

Yes. There's no legal limit on personal jewellery for residents. For amounts above SAR 60,000 equivalent (about 200g of 22K), you must declare at customs on departure — same rule as cash. Carry your purchase receipts to avoid questions at the destination customs.

How do I calculate Zakat on my gold jewellery?

Multiply your gold weight (in grams) by the current 24K price per gram (regardless of your jewellery's karat — for Zakat the gold-content value is used). If the total exceeds the Nisab (currently about 85g × 24K price = around SAR 25,000+), pay 2.5% Zakat annually. Use our Gold Zakat Calculator for a karat-aware calculation.

Why does the price quoted online differ from my local shop?

Three reasons: (1) the wholesale spot price changes every minute — local shops update daily or twice daily; (2) the shop adds its margin (typically 1–3% on gold content); (3) the shop's making charge is on top. The wholesale online price is a benchmark, not a retail quote.

Is buying gold a good investment in Saudi Arabia?

For long-term wealth preservation (5+ years), gold has historically outpaced inflation. The catch in Saudi Arabia is the making-charge premium — for pure investment, buy 24K bullion or coins (zero VAT, minimal making) rather than jewellery. Many expats prefer gold over riyal savings because it's portable and stable in their home country's currency too.
