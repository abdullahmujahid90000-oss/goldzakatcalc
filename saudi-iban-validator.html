❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the format of a Saudi IBAN?
A Saudi IBAN is 24 characters long: SA + 2-digit checksum + 2-digit bank code + 18-digit account number (e.g., SA0380000000608010167519).
How to validate a Saudi IBAN?
To validate a Saudi IBAN: (1) Ensure it starts with 'SA', (2) Check it's exactly 24 characters, (3) Verify the checksum using MOD-97 algorithm, (4) Confirm the bank code matches an official Saudi bank.
What are the Saudi bank codes?
Official Saudi bank codes include: 80 (Al Rajhi), 40 (SNB), 15 (Alinma), 50 (Riyad Bank), 60 (SAB), 70 (Banque Saudi Fransi), 90 (Arab National Bank).
Can I validate international IBANs?
This validator focuses on Saudi IBANs (SA format). For international IBANs, use country-specific validators as formats vary by nation.
Where can I find my Saudi IBAN?
You can find your Saudi IBAN on your bank statement, mobile banking app, ATM receipt, or by contacting your bank's customer service.
Is IBAN the same as account number?
No, IBAN includes your account number plus country code, checksum, and bank code. Your actual account number is embedded within the IBAN.
Why is IBAN validation important?
IBAN validation prevents transfer errors, ensures funds reach the correct recipient, and is required for all domestic and international transfers in Saudi Arabia.
What happens if I enter a wrong IBAN?
Transfers to invalid IBANs will be rejected or returned, causing delays and potential fees. Always validate before sending money.
