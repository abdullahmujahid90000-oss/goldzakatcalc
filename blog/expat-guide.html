🌍 Expat Guide to Saudi Arabia 2026: Iqama, Visas & Essential Tips

📅 Updated January 2026  ·  ⏱ 10 min read  ·  🏷️ Expat Life

Saudi Arabia is home to over 13 million expatriate workers — roughly 38% of the total population. Whether you're arriving for the first time or renewing your stay, understanding the rules around Iqama, dependent levies, and exit visas is essential for a smooth experience.

The Iqama (Residency Permit)

The Iqama (إقامة) is the official residency permit that all non-Saudi residents must carry. Key facts:

  • Your employer is responsible for issuing your initial Iqama within 90 days of arrival
  • Iqamas are typically renewed annually or biannually
  • Always carry your Iqama — police checks are common
  • Renewal must be done before expiry to avoid fines (SAR 500–1,000 per day for overstay)
  • Use our Iqama Expiry Calculator to track your renewal date
📅 Track your Iqama expiry: Enter your Iqama expiry date in Hijri (as printed on the card) and our calculator will show you exactly how many days remain and when to start the renewal process.

Dependent Levy (رسوم المرافقين)

Since 2017, expats pay a monthly levy for each dependent family member (spouse and children) on their Iqama:

  • Rate: SAR 400 per dependent per month (SAR 4,800/year per dependent)
  • Paid by the sponsor (the working family member)
  • Children under 18 are counted as dependents
  • Spouses who work independently are not dependents on your Iqama

Use our Dependent Levy Calculator to see your total annual cost.

Exit and Re-Entry Visas

Expats need an exit visa to leave Saudi Arabia — either single or multiple:

  • Single exit: Valid for 30 days from issue date — use it or lose it
  • Multiple exit: Valid for 90 days, allows multiple trips during that period
  • Exit visas are now issued by employers through Absher
  • The Premium Residency (Iqama Premium) holders can exit without employer permission

Key Apps Every Expat Needs

  • Absher (أبشر): Government services portal — renew Iqama, apply for exit visas, update information
  • Tawakkalna: Health and emergency services app
  • Musaned: For hiring and managing domestic workers
  • GOSI App: Check your social insurance status
  • STC Pay / Al Rajhi App: Mobile banking and remittances
