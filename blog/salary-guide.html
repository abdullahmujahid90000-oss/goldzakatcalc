💰 Saudi Salary Guide 2026: Minimum Wage, Allowances & Net Pay

📅 Updated January 2026  ·  ⏱ 7 min read  ·  🏷️ Labour Law

Understanding how salaries work in Saudi Arabia is crucial whether you're negotiating a new job offer, checking if your employer is paying correctly, or planning your finances. This guide covers everything from minimum wage to how GOSI affects your take-home pay.

Minimum Wage in Saudi Arabia 2026

Saudi Arabia has a tiered minimum wage structure:

  • Saudi nationals (private sector): SAR 4,000/month minimum (includes basic + housing)
  • Government sector (Saudi nationals): SAR 4,000/month base, plus cost of living allowance
  • Expatriate workers: No statutory minimum — subject to contract and Saudization requirements

Note: Many industries have sector-specific minimum wage agreements that may be higher.

How to Calculate Your Net (Take-Home) Salary

For Saudi Nationals:
Net Salary = Basic + Housing + Transport + Other Allowances − GOSI (9.75% of Basic + Housing)

For Expats:
Net Salary = Basic + Housing + Transport + Other (no GOSI deduction from salary)
Employer still pays 2% GOSI for expat occupational hazards — not deducted from employee

Overtime Pay Rules

Under Saudi Labour Law:

  • Standard working hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week
  • During Ramadan: 6 hours/day for Muslim employees
  • Overtime rate: 1.5× the hourly basic wage
  • Hourly rate = Basic Salary ÷ (8 hours × 26 working days)

Annual Leave Entitlement

  • Less than 5 years service: 21 days paid leave per year
  • 5 or more years service: 30 days paid leave per year
  • Leave pay is calculated on basic salary only
