How Salaries Work in Saudi Arabia

Saudi Arabia's private-sector compensation system has several unique characteristics that differ from Western countries. The most notable: no personal income tax. What you negotiate is (largely) what you take home. This makes the Kingdom attractive to professionals worldwide.

However, the salary structure itself is multi-component — and the split between components matters more than most employees realise. Your GOSI deduction, your end-of-service gratuity, and your housing entitlement all depend on how your total package is divided between basic salary, housing, and other allowances.

Why the Basic Salary Split Matters

Consider two employees both earning SAR 15,000/month total:

  • Employee A: SAR 9,000 basic + SAR 4,000 housing + SAR 2,000 other → EOS calculated on SAR 9,000
  • Employee B: SAR 6,000 basic + SAR 6,000 housing + SAR 3,000 other → EOS calculated on SAR 6,000

Over 10 years, the difference in EOS gratuity can be SAR 30,000+. Always negotiate a higher basic salary ratio, especially for long-term employment. Use our EOS Calculator to see the impact.

Minimum Wage in Saudi Arabia 2025

Saudi national minimum wage (private sector): SAR 4,000/month
There is no statutory minimum wage for expatriates — their salary is set by employment contract.

The SAR 4,000 minimum applies only to Saudi nationals in the private sector, introduced as part of Saudization initiatives. Many Saudi nationals in the private sector earn significantly above this — the minimum is primarily a floor for entry-level positions.

Government-sector Saudi employees operate under a different pay scale and generally earn more, with job security and benefits. Average government salaries for Saudi nationals are estimated at SAR 8,000–12,000/month for mid-level roles.

GOSI Deductions — What Comes Out of Your Pay

For Saudi nationals, the GOSI deduction is 9.75% of their basic salary + housing allowance. This is the employee's contribution to the GOSI retirement and SANED unemployment insurance.

📋 Saudi employee example: SAR 8,000 basic + SAR 3,000 housing

GOSI base = SAR 11,000
Monthly deduction = 11,000 × 9.75% = SAR 1,072.50
Net salary = SAR 15,000 − 1,072.50 = SAR 13,927.50

For expatriates, the GOSI deduction is only 2% of basic + housing (work injury insurance only).

📋 Expatriate example: SAR 8,000 basic + SAR 3,000 housing

GOSI base = SAR 11,000
Monthly deduction = 11,000 × 2% = SAR 220
Net salary = SAR 15,000 − 220 = SAR 14,780

Overtime Rules in Saudi Arabia

Saudi Labour Law specifies clear rules for overtime pay:

  • Standard working hours: 8 hours per day / 48 hours per week
  • Ramadan hours: Reduced to 6 hours per day for Muslim employees
  • Overtime rate: 150% of normal hourly wage (time and a half)
  • Maximum overtime: No specific statutory cap, but must be agreed by the employee
  • Friday (weekend): If required to work on Friday, employee must receive a day off in lieu or pay at 150%+

📋 Overtime calculation: SAR 8,000 basic salary

Monthly hours = 8 hrs × 26 working days = 208 hours
Hourly rate = 8,000 ÷ 208 = SAR 38.46/hour
Overtime rate = SAR 38.46 × 1.5 = SAR 57.69/hour
For 20 overtime hours: 20 × 57.69 = SAR 1,153.80 extra

Annual Leave and Leave Pay

Saudi Labour Law mandates annual leave based on years of service:

Service DurationAnnual Leave Entitlement
Less than 5 years21 days per year
5 years or more30 days per year

Leave must be paid at the employee's full salary (including all allowances, not just basic). Untaken leave at the end of employment must be paid out in cash.

Salary Protection System (SPS)

Saudi Arabia's Wage Protection System (WPS) — also called the Salary Protection System — mandates that all private-sector employers pay salaries electronically through approved channels by the specified date. This system:

  • Ensures employees are paid on time (employers must pay within the first 10 days of the following month)
  • Creates a digital trail of all salary payments, accessible by the Ministry of Human Resources
  • Allows employees to report late or missing payments via the HRSD portal
  • Non-compliant employers face service suspensions from government bodies

If your employer is regularly late with salary payments, you can file a complaint via the HRSD portal (hrsd.gov.sa) or by calling 19911.

Saudization (Nitaqat) and Its Salary Impact

Nitaqat is Saudi Arabia's mandatory nationalization programme. Private companies must maintain a minimum percentage of Saudi national employees, with different quotas by industry and company size. Companies are classified into bands:

BandStatusImplications
PlatinumExcellentMaximum flexibility, can hire from Red zone companies
High GreenAbove averageStandard hiring privileges
Low GreenCompliantStandard hiring, some restrictions
YellowBelow requiredCannot renew expat visas, limited new hiring
RedNon-compliantSerious restrictions, fines, cannot sponsor new workers

Nitaqat creates strong demand for qualified Saudi nationals, driving up salaries for Saudis with in-demand skills. Companies in Red/Yellow bands sometimes offer premium salaries to Saudi candidates to improve their Nitaqat score quickly.

Housing and Other Benefits

Beyond the monthly salary, many employers in Saudi Arabia provide additional benefits that significantly enhance total compensation:

  • Company housing: Common in remote industrial locations (refineries, construction camps). Saves the employee the entire housing allowance.
  • Annual air ticket: Many expat contracts include one annual economy-class air ticket to the employee's home country.
  • Medical insurance: Mandatory for all employees and their families under Saudi law. Quality varies widely — from basic clinic access to comprehensive family plans.
  • School fees: Senior expat packages sometimes include private school fees for children.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum wage in Saudi Arabia in 2025? +
The minimum wage for Saudi nationals in the private sector is SAR 4,000 per month as of 2025. There is no statutory minimum wage for expatriates — their salary is set by employment contract.
What are the standard salary components in Saudi Arabia? +
A typical Saudi private-sector package includes: basic salary (40-60% of total), housing allowance (25-30%), transport allowance (10-15%), and sometimes food allowance, mobile allowance, and performance bonuses. The split matters because GOSI is calculated only on basic + housing, and EOS only on basic salary.
How is overtime calculated in Saudi Arabia? +
Overtime is paid at 150% of the hourly wage for hours beyond 8 per day or 48 per week. Hourly rate = basic salary ÷ 208 (standard monthly hours). During Ramadan, the standard is 6 hours/day for Muslim employees.
Do expatriates pay income tax on salary in Saudi Arabia? +
No. Saudi Arabia does not impose personal income tax. Both Saudi nationals and expatriates receive salaries free of income tax. Check your home country's rules for any worldwide income tax obligations.
What is Nitaqat and how does it affect salaries? +
Nitaqat is Saudi Arabia's Saudization quota system requiring private companies to employ a minimum percentage of Saudi nationals. It creates strong demand for qualified Saudis, driving up their market salaries. Companies failing to meet quotas face hiring restrictions.
What deductions come out of my Saudi salary? +
For Saudi nationals: GOSI 9.75% on basic + housing. For expatriates: GOSI 2% on basic + housing. No income tax for anyone. Other deductions (loans, advances) only as contractually agreed.
