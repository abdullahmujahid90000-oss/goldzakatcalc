📋 Complete Guide to End of Service Benefits in Saudi Arabia 2026

📅 January 2026 ⏱️ 8 min read 🏷️ Labour Law Updated for Saudi Labour Law 2026

What is End of Service (EOS)?

End of Service benefits (مكافأة نهاية الخدمة), also called gratuity, is a mandatory payment that Saudi employers must make to workers when their employment ends. It is governed by Article 84 of the Saudi Labour Law and applies to all workers — Saudi and non-Saudi — employed under the Labour Law.

EOS is one of the most important financial rights of workers in Saudi Arabia, and many employees are unaware of how much they are entitled to — or that they may be receiving less than what they deserve.

Who is Entitled to EOS?

All workers covered under the Saudi Labour Law are entitled to EOS, including:

  • Saudi nationals in private sector employment
  • Expatriate workers on Iqama-sponsored by a private employer
  • Domestic workers (with separate protections under Domestic Workers Law)

Not covered: Government employees (who have separate pension systems), casual workers, and workers employed for less than 2 years who resign voluntarily.

Article 84: The EOS Formula

Under Article 84, EOS is calculated based on your last basic salary (not including allowances) and your years of service:

Years 1–5: ½ monthly salary × number of years
Years 6+: 1 full monthly salary × number of additional years

Add both amounts for your total EOS entitlement (if termination). For resignation, see the table below.

Resignation vs. Termination: Key Differences

Your reason for leaving significantly affects your EOS entitlement:

🚪 If You Resign (Voluntary Resignation)

  • Less than 2 years: No EOS entitlement
  • 2–5 years: 1/3 of EOS amount
  • 5–10 years: 2/3 of EOS amount
  • 10+ years: Full EOS amount (same as termination)

❌ If You Are Terminated (by employer)

  • Full EOS regardless of years of service
  • If terminated for cause (Article 80), you may still be entitled to EOS but not end-of-service notice pay
  • If terminated for redundancy (Article 74/75), you are entitled to full EOS + notice pay

Worked Example: EOS Calculation

📊 Example: 7 Years of Service, SAR 8,000 Basic Salary, Terminated

First 5 years: ½ × SAR 8,000 × 5 = SAR 20,000

Years 6–7 (2 additional years): 1 × SAR 8,000 × 2 = SAR 16,000

Total EOS: SAR 36,000

If the same employee had resigned with 7 years of service, they would receive 2/3 × SAR 36,000 = SAR 24,000

What Counts as "Basic Salary"?

EOS is calculated on the basic salary only — not total package. The basic salary is the fixed monthly payment stated in your employment contract, excluding:

  • Housing allowance
  • Transportation allowance
  • Food allowance
  • Bonuses and commissions (unless contractually guaranteed)
  • Overtime pay

Always check your employment contract to identify your basic salary clearly.

When Must EOS Be Paid?

Saudi Labour Law requires employers to pay EOS within 10 days of the employment contract ending. If your employer delays beyond this, you have the right to file a complaint with the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Development (MHRSD).

What If Your Employer Refuses to Pay?

If you believe you are owed EOS and your employer is refusing, follow these steps:

  1. First, try to resolve it directly with your HR department in writing
  2. File a complaint online at musaned.com.sa or the MHRSD portal
  3. Contact the Labour Court (Mahkama Al-Amal) for legal action
  4. For expats: Contact your embassy or consulate for additional support

EOS and GOSI: How They Interact

Saudi employees who have been enrolled in GOSI for the required number of years may be eligible for GOSI pension instead of — or in addition to — EOS depending on the employment end scenario. For Saudi nationals, GOSI pension is the primary retirement benefit, and EOS may or may not apply depending on GOSI enrollment years. Consult an HR specialist if you are a Saudi national approaching retirement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does EOS apply to part-time workers?

Yes, part-time workers enrolled in the Saudi Labour Law system are entitled to EOS on a pro-rated basis based on their working hours relative to full-time employment.

Is EOS taxable?

For non-Saudi employees, EOS is generally not subject to Saudi income tax (as Saudi Arabia doesn't impose income tax on wages). However, you may have tax obligations in your home country — consult a local tax advisor.

Can my employer reduce my EOS?

No. The minimum EOS amounts under Article 84 are statutory floors. Your employer cannot legally pay you less than the minimum, although they can voluntarily pay more.
