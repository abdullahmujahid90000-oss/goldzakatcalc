🥇 Gold Zakat in Saudi Arabia 2026: Complete Guide

📅 Updated January 2026  ·  ⏱ 7 min read  ·  🏷️ Zakat

Zakat on gold is one of the pillars of Islamic finance and one of the most commonly asked questions among Muslims in Saudi Arabia. Whether you own gold jewelry, bullion, or coins, understanding when and how much Zakat is due is essential.

The Nisab for Gold: The Threshold That Triggers Zakat

Zakat on gold only becomes obligatory when your total gold holdings reach the Nisab — the minimum threshold. The Nisab for gold is 85 grams of pure (24-karat) gold.

Does Jewelry Count? The Scholarly Debate

There is a well-known scholarly difference of opinion on gold jewelry:

  • Majority view (Hanafi, Hanbali, Maliki): Zakat is obligatory on all gold, including jewelry worn regularly
  • Shafi'i view: No Zakat on jewelry intended for personal use

In Saudi Arabia, many scholars follow the majority view. To be safe, many Muslims calculate Zakat on all gold they own.

The Zakat Formula for Gold

Step 1: Convert all gold to pure (24K) equivalent grams
   Pure weight = Actual weight × Purity fraction

Step 2: Check if total pure gold ≥ 85 grams (Nisab)

Step 3: If yes: Zakat = Total gold value × 2.5%
   Total value = Pure weight × Current 24K price per gram

Worked Example

Suppose you own 120 grams of 21K gold:

  • Pure gold = 120 × 0.875 = 105 grams (above 85g Nisab ✅)
  • Value = 105 × SAR 320 = SAR 33,600
  • Zakat due = SAR 33,600 × 2.5% = SAR 840

The Haul Condition: One Full Islamic Year

Zakat on gold is only due if you have owned gold above the Nisab continuously for one full Islamic (Hijri) year. If you purchased gold mid-year, start counting from the date your holdings first reached the Nisab.

Gold Units Conversion

  • 1 Tola (مثقال) = 4.25 grams
  • 1 Troy ounce = 31.1 grams
  • 1 kilogram = 1,000 grams
