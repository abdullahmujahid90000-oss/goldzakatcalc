Iqama Renewal Guide 2025: Fees, Step-by-Step Process, Fines & Job Transfer Rules

Everything expatriates need to know about renewing their Saudi Iqama — including costs, how to do it through Absher, and what happens if it expires.

What is an Iqama?

The Iqama (إقامة — literally "residence") is the official residency permit issued to all non-Saudi nationals living in the Kingdom. It is your primary government-issued ID in Saudi Arabia and the document that legally authorises your presence in the country.

Without a valid, active Iqama, an expatriate cannot legally work, drive, open a bank account, rent an apartment, access most government services, travel, or register their children in school. The Iqama is not just a document — it is the foundation of your entire legal status in Saudi Arabia.

Iqamas are issued for periods of 1 or 2 years and must be renewed before expiry. The renewal process is now largely digital through the Absher and Muqeem platforms.

Who pays the Iqama fee? By Saudi Labour Law, the employer is responsible for all Iqama-related fees and renewals for their employees. However, many employment contracts — especially in smaller companies — include clauses passing this cost to the employee. Check your contract carefully.

Step-by-Step Iqama Renewal Process

1

Check expiry date (1–3 months before)

Log into Absher Individuals app or check via our Iqama Expiry Calculator. Iqamas are dated in Hijri calendar — we convert it to Gregorian for you.

2

Employer logs into Absher Business

The HR manager or PRO (Public Relations Officer) logs into the Absher Business portal using the company's credentials.

3

Navigate to Iqama Renewal

Absher Business → Establishments Services → Renew Iqama. Select the employee from the company's registered workers list.

4

Pay the renewal fee

SAR 650 is paid online via SADAD payment system or credit/debit card. The system will also show any outstanding fines or fees.

5

Iqama renewed digitally

Upon successful payment, the Iqama is renewed in the system. The employee can view the updated digital Iqama in their Absher Individuals app within minutes.

6

Physical card update (if needed)

If a new physical Iqama card is required (e.g., the old one is damaged or expired), visit a Jawazat (Passports) office or use the self-service kiosks available at major Jawazat branches.

Saudi Arabia periodically offers amnesty periods allowing undocumented or overstaying residents to regularise their status or exit voluntarily with reduced or waived fines. Watch for official announcements from the Ministry of Interior.

Employer liability: If your Iqama expires because your employer failed to renew it, the fines are legally the employer's responsibility. Document all communications requesting renewal. If the employer refuses, you can file a complaint via the Qiwa platform or HRSD.

Checking Iqama Status and Expiry

Method 1: Absher App

Download Absher Individuals from App Store or Google Play. Log in with your Iqama number and mobile OTP. Go to "My Services" → "Iqama information" to see your current status, expiry date, and any pending issues.

Method 2: Muqeem Portal

Visit muqeem.sa and log in with your credentials. The Muqeem portal shows detailed residency information including dependents registered under your Iqama.

Method 3: Iqama Expiry Calculator

Iqama expiry dates are shown in the Hijri (Islamic) calendar. Use our Iqama Expiry Calculator — enter the Hijri date from your Iqama card and instantly see the Gregorian equivalent so you know exactly when to renew.

Job Transfer — Moving to a New Employer

One of the landmark reforms of Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 labour programme was the introduction of free job mobility for most private-sector expatriates in 2021. Previously, changing employers required the original employer's consent — a significant source of worker exploitation.

Current Job Transfer Rules (2025)

  • Eligible: Most private-sector expatriates who have completed one year of service with their current employer
  • Process: Via the Qiwa platform (qiwa.sa) — the new employer initiates the transfer request
  • Without employer consent: If you have completed 1 year, you can transfer without the original employer agreeing — they cannot block you
  • Before 1 year: Transfer still possible but requires employer approval or a valid reason (employer contract violation, non-payment of salary, etc.)
  • Domestic workers: Different rules apply; managed through Musaned platform

Transfer Process via Qiwa

  1. New employer registers on Qiwa and initiates transfer request
  2. You receive notification via Absher and must approve the transfer
  3. Original employer is notified (cannot block if 1-year condition met)
  4. Iqama sponsorship transferred to new employer's name
  5. New employment contract registered on Qiwa
Protect yourself during transfer: Do not resign or leave your current employer until the Qiwa transfer is fully completed and your Iqama shows the new sponsor. Leaving without completing the transfer can leave you in an undocumented status.

Iqama for Dependents

If your family (spouse, children, parents) lives with you in Saudi Arabia, each family member needs their own Iqama as a dependent. Managing dependent Iqamas:

  • Dependent Iqamas are linked to the primary Iqama holder's sponsorship
  • Dependent Iqamas must also be renewed annually — done through Muqeem portal
  • The dependent levy (SAR 400/month per dependent) must be paid; failure to pay results in Iqama suspension
  • Children born in Saudi Arabia receive an Iqama at birth — register promptly at Jawazat
  • Dependent Iqamas are cancelled automatically if the primary holder's Iqama expires or is cancelled

Premium Residency (Iqama Mumayaza)

Saudi Arabia introduced a Premium Residency programme (الإقامة المميزة) — also called the "Green Card" — which offers permanent or long-term residency for qualifying high-net-worth individuals and skilled professionals. Key features:

  • Cost: SAR 800,000 for permanent premium residency (one-time), or SAR 100,000/year for renewable premium residency
  • Benefits: No employer sponsorship, freedom to work without Iqama renewal, own property, sponsor family independently
  • Eligibility: High-net-worth individuals, professionals in key sectors, investors
  • Application: Via the Premium Residency Centre (prc.gov.sa)

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to renew Iqama in Saudi Arabia? +
The standard Iqama renewal fee is SAR 650 per year for most expatriate workers. By law, employers are responsible for this fee, though some contracts pass the cost to employees. Additional fines apply for late renewals.
How do I renew my Iqama in Saudi Arabia? +
Iqama renewal is done through the Absher Business portal by the employer. Steps: 1) Log into Absher Business. 2) Go to Establishments Services → Iqama Renewal. 3) Pay SAR 650 via SADAD. 4) Iqama is updated digitally — view in your Absher Individuals app.
What are the fines for an expired Iqama in Saudi Arabia? +
Fines start at SAR 500 for the first month of delay, increasing to SAR 1,000+ for 2–3 months, SAR 2,000+ for 3–6 months, and SAR 5,000+ for prolonged overstay. Deportation and re-entry bans are possible for serious cases.
Can I change jobs with an Iqama in Saudi Arabia? +
Yes. Private-sector expatriates who have completed one year of service can transfer jobs without employer permission via the Qiwa platform. The new employer initiates the transfer; the original employer cannot block it once 1 year is completed.
How do I check my Iqama expiry date? +
Check via: 1) Absher Individuals app → My Services → Iqama information. 2) Muqeem portal (muqeem.sa). 3) Our Iqama Expiry Calculator — enter the Hijri date from your Iqama card to get the Gregorian equivalent.
What is the difference between Iqama and Muqeem? +
Iqama is the residency permit document. Muqeem (مقيم) is the Saudi government's online platform for managing residency services including renewals, dependent registration, and status inquiries. You manage your Iqama through the Muqeem platform.
