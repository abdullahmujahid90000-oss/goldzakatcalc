🪪 Iqama Guide 2026: Renewal, Fees & What Happens When It Expires

📅 Updated January 2026  ·  ⏱ 7 min read  ·  🏷️ Expat Life

The Iqama (إقامة) is the residence permit that allows expatriates to legally live and work in Saudi Arabia. Every expat and their dependents must have a valid Iqama at all times. This guide explains everything you need to know — from how to track your expiry to what fines apply if it lapses.

What Is an Iqama?

The Iqama is an identity card issued by the Saudi Ministry of Interior (through the Jawazat — General Directorate of Passports) to all non-Saudi residents. It contains your personal details, employer information, and — critically — the expiry date displayed in the Hijri (Islamic) calendar.

The expiry date is in Hijri format (e.g., 15/06/1447H). Use our Iqama Expiry Calculator to convert it to a Gregorian date and see exactly how many days remain.

How to Renew Your Iqama

  • Renewal is done via Absher (أبشر) — the government digital services platform
  • Employers (HR department) typically handle renewals through the Muqeem system
  • Documents needed: Current Iqama, passport, employer's CR, employee contract
  • Biometric data (fingerprints) must be updated at a Jawazat office every 5 years
  • Start the renewal process at least 1 month before expiry

Fines for Expired Iqama

⚠️ Fine for expired Iqama: SAR 500 per day for the first year of overstay, then higher penalties for continued overstay. Deportation is possible for long-term overstays.
  • Holding an expired Iqama is a deportable offense
  • Employers who fail to renew employee Iqamas can be fined and blacklisted
  • You cannot exit Saudi Arabia on an expired Iqama without paying accumulated fines
  • An "amnesty exit" programme is occasionally offered for those wishing to self-deport — check MOI announcements

Reading Your Iqama Expiry Date

Your Iqama shows the expiry in Hijri format, e.g., 15/06/1447 means the 15th of Jumada al-Thani, 1447H. To find the Gregorian equivalent:

  • Use our free Iqama Expiry Calculator
  • Or check the Absher app — it shows both Hijri and Gregorian dates

Iqama Colour Coding

Saudi Iqamas come in two colours: Green for Saudi nationals and their families, and Blue for expatriates. The colour matters in some contexts (hospital registration, school admission).

Transferring Your Iqama (Job Change)

Moving to a new employer requires a transfer of Iqama sponsorship. As of 2021, Saudi Arabia's Labour Reform Initiative (LRI) has made it easier for expats to change jobs without explicit employer approval, provided they have worked for their current employer for at least one year and meet other conditions.
