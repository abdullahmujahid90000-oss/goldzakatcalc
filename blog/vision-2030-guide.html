🇸🇦 Saudi Vision 2030: What It Means for Workers, Expats & Businesses

📅 Updated January 2026  ·  ⏱ 8 min read  ·  🏷️ Economy

Saudi Vision 2030 (رؤية المملكة 2030) is Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman's ambitious blueprint to transform the Saudi economy and society by 2030. Announced in April 2016, it represents the most sweeping set of changes to the Kingdom in modern history — affecting everything from entertainment and tourism to labour markets and women's rights.

What Vision 2030 Means for Expat Workers

For the 13+ million expat workers in Saudi Arabia, Vision 2030 brings both opportunities and challenges:

  • Nitaqat (Saudization) is intensifying: More sectors are requiring higher percentages of Saudi employees. Companies below their Saudization target cannot issue new work visas or renew existing ones
  • Labour Reform Initiative (LRI): Expats can now change jobs more freely, apply for exit visas without employer approval, and access new legal protections
  • New visa categories: Tourist visas, Premium Residency, and seasonal worker permits have made the visa system more flexible
  • Dependent levy remains: SAR 400/month per dependent is likely to continue as a way to manage expat population levels

Saudization (Nitaqat) — Sector Requirements

Nitaqat requires companies to maintain minimum percentages of Saudi nationals. Compliance determines a company's rating (Platinum, Green, Yellow, Red):

  • High-pressure sectors: Retail, hospitality, tourism, HR services — very high Saudization targets
  • Technical sectors: IT, engineering, healthcare — moderate targets with skills-based exemptions
  • Oil & Gas: Different rules apply under ARAMCO and major contractors
  • Companies in the Red zone cannot renew expat visas and face penalties

Major Vision 2030 Projects Creating Jobs

  • NEOM: $500B+ futuristic city project on the Red Sea — thousands of specialist roles
  • Red Sea Project: Ultra-luxury tourism development — hospitality, construction
  • Diriyah Gate: Cultural heritage tourism project near Riyadh
  • Qiddiya: Entertainment city including Six Flags theme park
  • Saudi Aramco Expansion: Continued energy infrastructure investment

Lifestyle Changes Under Vision 2030

  • Cinemas, concerts, and mixed-gender entertainment now permitted
  • Tourism visas available for 49+ nationalities — on-arrival or e-visa
  • Women can drive (since 2018), travel independently, and hold more professional roles
  • Public concerts, sports events (F1, WWE, golf) now regular
  • Dress code enforcement has relaxed significantly in public spaces

What to Watch for Workers & Businesses

The pace of change means rules are updating regularly. Key things to monitor include Nitaqat percentage changes, new fee structures, and updates to the Labour Reform Initiative. Always verify current requirements through official HRSD (Ministry of Human Resources) channels.
