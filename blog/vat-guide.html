🧾 VAT in Saudi Arabia 2026: Complete Guide to the 15% Tax

📅 Updated January 2026  ·  ⏱ 6 min read  ·  🏷️ Finance

Value Added Tax (VAT) was introduced in Saudi Arabia in January 2018 at 5%, then tripled to 15% in July 2020 as part of the Kingdom's fiscal reform under Vision 2030. Understanding how VAT works is essential for consumers, businesses, and expats in Saudi Arabia.

Current VAT Rate

15%

The standard VAT rate of 15% applies to most goods and services sold in Saudi Arabia. This rate has been in effect since 1 July 2020 and is administered by the Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA — هيئة الزكاة والضريبة والجمارك).

How to Calculate VAT

Adding VAT to a price:
VAT Amount = Price × 15%  |  Total = Price × 1.15

Removing VAT from a total (reverse VAT):
Original Price = Total ÷ 1.15  |  VAT Amount = Total - Original Price

Example: A product costs SAR 1,000 before VAT. VAT = SAR 150. Total = SAR 1,150.

Zero-Rated vs. Exempt Goods

✅ Zero-Rated (0% VAT)

Exports outside GCC, international transport, qualifying financial services, crude oil & petroleum exports

🔴 Exempt (No VAT)

Residential property rentals, bare land, local passenger transport, financial services (margins/commissions)

Note: Basic food items are no longer zero-rated — they became subject to the full 15% rate since July 2020.

VAT Registration Requirements

  • Mandatory: Businesses with annual taxable supplies exceeding SAR 375,000
  • Voluntary: Businesses with supplies between SAR 187,500 and SAR 375,000
  • Registration is done through ZATCA's online portal (zatca.gov.sa)
  • Non-compliance fines: Up to SAR 10,000 for failure to register

VAT Filing & Payment

Registered businesses must file VAT returns either monthly or quarterly depending on their annual revenue:

  • Revenue ≥ SAR 40 million/year: Monthly filing (due by the 30th of the following month)
  • Revenue < SAR 40 million/year: Quarterly filing (due within 30 days of quarter end)

Real Estate & VAT

A special 15% VAT applies to commercial real estate sales and rentals. Residential property rentals remain exempt. The first home purchase by Saudi nationals may qualify for a government VAT rebate under specific conditions — check ZATCA's website for eligibility.

VAT Invoicing Requirements

All VAT-registered businesses must issue compliant e-invoices (FATOORAH — فاتورة) through ZATCA's approved systems. The invoice must include: the seller's VAT number, buyer details for B2B, VAT amount, and total including VAT.
