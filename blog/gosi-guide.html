GOSI Saudi Arabia 2026: The Complete Guide to Social Insurance

📅 Updated January 2026  ·  ⏱ 8 min read  ·  🏷️ Labour Law

The General Organization for Social Insurance (GOSI — التأمينات الاجتماعية) is Saudi Arabia's mandatory social security system covering retirement, disability, death, and occupational hazards. Both Saudi and non-Saudi employees may be covered, though the scope differs.

Who Must Register with GOSI?

Enrollment is mandatory for:

  • Saudi nationals working in the private sector — fully covered for all branches
  • Expatriate workers — covered for occupational hazards only (employer pays 2%)
  • Employers with one or more employees must register within 10 days of hiring

How Is the GOSI Base Calculated?

GOSI Contribution Base = Basic Salary + Housing Allowance
Employee deduction = Contribution Base × 9.75%
Employer contribution = Contribution Base × 11.75%

Note: Not all allowances are included. Transportation, food, and phone allowances are generally excluded from the GOSI base.

Maximum & Minimum Salary Ceiling

GOSI applies a maximum monthly salary ceiling of SAR 45,000 — contributions are not calculated on salary above this amount. There is no minimum ceiling for private-sector employees.

When Are Contributions Paid?

Employers must pay GOSI contributions by the 15th of the following month. Late payments incur a 2% monthly penalty on the overdue amount. All payments are made through the GOSI online portal (gosi.gov.sa).

Expatriate Coverage: Occupational Hazards Only

Expat workers are covered solely under the Occupational Hazards branch. This means:

  • No deduction from the expat employee's salary
  • The employer pays 2% of the employee's basic + housing salary
  • Coverage includes work injuries, occupational diseases, and death resulting from work
  • Compensation is a multiple of the employee's monthly wage, up to SAR 522,000

Benefits for Saudi Employees

Retirement Pension

Saudi employees become eligible for a retirement pension after accumulating at least 300 months (25 years) of contributions or reaching age 60 with at least 10 years of contributions. The pension is calculated as a percentage of the average final wage.

Disability Benefits

Covered employees who become permanently disabled receive a monthly pension. The disability must be certified by GOSI's medical board.

Death Benefits

If a covered employee dies, eligible dependents (spouse, children, parents) receive monthly survivor pensions based on the deceased's contribution history.

How to Register with GOSI

Employers register online at gosi.gov.sa. Required documents include the commercial registration, owner/manager ID, and details of all employees. Employees are then enrolled through the employer portal. Fines for non-registration start at SAR 5,000.

GOSI & End of Service (EOS)

GOSI contributions reduce the End of Service gratuity obligation for employers with Saudi staff, as GOSI replaces part of the social safety net. However, EOS gratuity under Saudi Labour Law Article 84 is still paid separately — GOSI does not replace it.
