Dependent Levy in Saudi Arabia 2026 — Complete Guide to Fees & Exemptions

Everything you need to know about the dependent levy (the fee charged for expatriates' family members), who pays it, who is exempt, and how to calculate your annual cost.

Calculate now: use the free Dependent Levy Calculator to get your exact cost.

What is the dependent levy?

The dependent levy is a monthly fee paid for each family member living in Saudi Arabia on a dependent (family) visa. It was introduced in 2017 as part of the Vision 2030 fiscal reforms, and currently stands at SAR 400 per month for each dependent.

Who is exempt from the dependent levy?

  • Dependents of Saudi citizens — fully exempt
  • Dependents of employees at certain government entities — check with your employer
  • Anyone holding their own work visa — they are not counted as a dependent
  • Domestic workers (drivers, housemaids) — a different fee system applies

Dependent levy vs. iqama renewal fee — the difference

These are two completely different fees. The iqama renewal fee (around SAR 650/year) is paid once a year to renew your residency document. The dependent levy (SAR 400/month) is an ongoing monthly fee that does not renew anything — it is simply a residency charge for each dependent. If you have family in the Kingdom, you must pay both.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is the monthly dependent levy in Saudi Arabia 2026? ▼
The dependent levy is SAR 400 per month for each dependent. A family of a spouse and two children = SAR 1,200/month = SAR 14,400/year.
Is the employer required to pay the dependent levy? ▼
By regulation the sponsor (employer) is primarily liable. However, many employment contracts transfer this obligation to the employee. Check your contract before bringing your family.
What happens if I don't pay the dependent levy? ▼
Unpaid fees accumulate as a debt on your residency record. You may be unable to renew your iqama or issue an exit visa until the debt is settled. Pay via Absher or your employer's Muqeem account.
