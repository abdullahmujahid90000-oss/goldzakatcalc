About Exit Re-entry Visas

What is it? An exit re-entry visa allows an expat worker to leave Saudi Arabia and return within the visa validity period.

Single exit visa: Allows one trip outside KSA. Once you return to KSA, the visa expires.

Multiple exit visa: Allows multiple trips within the validity period (6 months, 1 year, or 2 years).

How to get it: Apply through Absher (absher.sa) or through your employer's HR. Some visas can be issued online.

Important: If your Iqama expires while you're outside KSA, you may face difficulties returning. Always ensure your Iqama is valid for the duration of your trip.

Saudi Arabia Exit & Re-entry Visa 2026 — Costs, Rules & Common Mistakes

Every expat in Saudi Arabia under standard work-visa sponsorship needs an Exit & Re-entry visa (تأشيرة خروج وعودة) before leaving the Kingdom temporarily — for holiday, family visits, medical treatment or business trips. Forgetting to issue it, choosing the wrong duration, or letting it expire while abroad creates real problems: cancelled Iqama, blocked re-entry, even fines paid by your employer that may be deducted from your salary. This calculator tells you the exact cost. The guide below covers everything that's not in the price.

Single-exit vs Multiple-exit — which one you actually need

There are two flavours of Saudi exit visa: single-exit (you leave and re-enter once) and multiple-exit (you leave and re-enter as many times as you want within the validity period). The 2026 fees are SAR 200 for a 2-month single-exit, SAR 100 for each additional month, and SAR 500 for a 3-month multiple-exit (with SAR 200 per additional month). The maximum total stay outside the Kingdom on any exit visa is 180 days; beyond that your Iqama is automatically cancelled and you must apply for a new work visa from your home country to return.

The hidden rule: the multiple-exit only makes financial sense if you travel two or more times within its validity. For a single annual holiday, the single-exit is cheaper. For a regional sales role with monthly trips to Dubai, multiple-exit pays for itself within two journeys.

Who pays — you or your employer?

By Saudi Labour Law, the employer must pay for the first work-related exit visa (annual home-leave). For all personal exits, the cost falls on the employee. The visa is processed exclusively by the sponsoring employer through Absher (Muqeem), so the employee has to formally request it from HR. The fee is deducted automatically from the company's Saudi bank account or your account, depending on the agreement in your contract.

Final Exit visa — completely different and much riskier

Don't confuse the Exit/Re-entry visa with a Final Exit visa. A Final Exit visa is for leaving Saudi Arabia permanently — it cancels your Iqama, you cannot return on the same sponsorship, and any outstanding loans or fines must be settled before it is issued. A Final Exit is what you use when you resign, complete your contract, or are deported. The Exit/Re-entry visa, by contrast, keeps your Iqama active and allows you to return to the same job.

Common mistakes that cost expats thousands

  • Letting the exit visa expire while abroad. Each day past the visa expiry costs an overstay penalty of SAR 100 per day. Stay 30 days too long = SAR 3,000 fine plus a 1-year re-entry ban.
  • Travelling with an expired Iqama. Your Iqama must be valid for the entire duration of the exit visa AND for 90 days after you return. Renew the Iqama BEFORE applying for exit. Check expiry with our Iqama Tracker.
  • Family on dependent visas without their own exit visa. Every dependent (spouse, child) on your Iqama needs their own exit visa, with their own fee. Forgetting a child's exit visa is the most common reason families get split up at the airport.
  • Applying too late. Standard processing is 24–48 hours but during peak Ramadan / summer-holiday season can take up to 5 working days. Apply at least one week before travel.
  • Cancelling exit visa unnecessarily. If you cancel an unused exit visa, the fee is non-refundable. Only apply when travel is confirmed.

How to apply on Absher (employer-driven, you initiate the request)

  1. Verify your Iqama validity covers your intended return date + 90 days. Renew if needed.
  2. Ask your HR / PRO to log into Absher Business (or Muqeem) and select your record.
  3. Choose Exit-Re-entry visa type (single or multiple), duration, and destination.
  4. HR pays the fee through Sadad. The exit visa is electronic — no paper sticker is needed.
  5. You receive a confirmation SMS. Save the exit visa reference number.
  6. At the airport, the immigration officer scans your Iqama and pulls up your active exit visa automatically.

Frequently asked questions

Can I extend the exit visa while I'm outside Saudi Arabia?

Yes — your employer can extend it via Absher Business while you're still abroad, provided the current visa hasn't yet expired and your Iqama remains valid. Extension fee is SAR 100 per additional month. The instant the original exit visa expires, extension is no longer possible and overstay fines begin.

What happens if my Iqama expires while I'm on exit visa?

If your Iqama expires during the exit period, your re-entry will be blocked and you'll need to coordinate Iqama renewal from abroad through your employer. Pay the renewal fee (SAR 650 plus any late penalty) via Sadad. After renewal, Absher will allow re-entry. Always check both Iqama AND exit visa dates before travel.

Do I need an exit visa if I have Premium Residency?

No. Holders of the Saudi Premium Residency (the SR 800,000 permanent visa or the renewable annual variant) can leave and re-enter the Kingdom freely without an exit/re-entry visa, similar to GCC citizens. This is one of the program's main benefits. See our Premium Residency Calculator.

Can I use the exit visa to visit any country?

Yes — the Saudi exit/re-entry visa only governs your re-entry to Saudi Arabia. Where you go in between is up to the visa policy of the destination country. You must of course also hold a valid passport and any required visa for that country.

My employer refuses to issue my exit visa — what do I do?

Under reformed labour rules since 2021, employers can no longer arbitrarily refuse an exit visa for personal travel. If your employer blocks it without reason, file a complaint at hrsd.gov.sa or the Labour Hotline 19911. Provide your contract and any written communication. HRSD typically rules in the employee's favour within 5 working days.
