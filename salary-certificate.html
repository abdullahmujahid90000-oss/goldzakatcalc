What is a Salary Certificate in Saudi Arabia? (شهادة راتب)

A salary certificate (شهادة راتب or خطاب راتب) is an official letter issued by an employer on company letterhead, confirming that a specific person is employed by the company and stating their monthly salary and allowances. It is one of the most frequently requested documents by banks, landlords, and government offices in Saudi Arabia.

📌 Salary Certificate vs Salary Slip: A salary certificate is a letter confirming employment and salary (used for third parties like banks). A salary slip is a monthly breakdown of earnings and deductions given to the employee. Both may be needed — banks often require both.

What Must a Saudi Salary Certificate Include?

  • Company letterhead — name, logo, CR number, address
  • Date of issue — must be recent (banks usually accept within 1–3 months)
  • Employee full name — exactly as on the Iqama
  • Iqama number or passport number
  • Job title and department
  • Employment start date and employment type (permanent/contract)
  • Salary breakdown — basic salary + all allowances + total monthly salary
  • Purpose statement — "This certificate is issued for the purpose of…"
  • Signature of authorized signatory (HR Manager or General Manager)
  • Company stamp / seal

How Long is a Salary Certificate Valid?

Most Saudi banks and landlords accept salary certificates issued within the last 30–90 days. For visa applications, embassies typically require a certificate dated within the last 3 months. For court or legal purposes, recency requirements vary. Always request a fresh certificate when in doubt — most HR departments issue them within 1–2 working days.

What is the difference between a salary certificate and an employment letter?

A salary certificate (شهادة راتب) specifically states the employee's salary and allowances. An employment letter (خطاب عمل) confirms employment status but may not include salary details. Banks always need the salary certificate — the employment letter alone is not sufficient for loan applications.

What salary is needed to rent an apartment in Saudi Arabia?

There is no official minimum, but landlords and real estate companies typically expect your monthly salary to be at least 3–4 times the monthly rent. So for an apartment renting at SAR 3,000/month, you would typically need a salary of SAR 9,000–12,000/month. Some landlords also accept rent-to-income ratios of 25–33%.

Can a salary certificate be in Arabic only?

For use within Saudi Arabia (banks, landlords, government offices), an Arabic-only certificate is fully acceptable. For embassy or foreign consulate submissions (e.g. to obtain a Pakistani, Indian or Filipino passport service), an English certificate or a bilingual certificate is preferred. Our generator produces an English certificate with Arabic elements built in.

How do I get my salary certificate if my employer is uncooperative?

Your employer is legally obligated to provide employment-related documentation. If your employer refuses, you can file a complaint with the Ministry of Human Resources at hrsd.gov.sa or call 19911. You can also request a letter from the GOSI system via Absher showing your registered salary — this is accepted by some banks as an alternative proof of income.
