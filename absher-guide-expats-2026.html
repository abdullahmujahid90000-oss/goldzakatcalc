Absher Guide 2026 — How to Use Absher as an Expat in Saudi Arabia

⚡ Quick Answer

Absher (أبشر) is Saudi Arabia's official government digital platform at absher.sa — available as a web portal and mobile app. Expats use it to renew Iqama, apply for exit/re-entry visas, track dependent status, update contact info, and access 165+ government services without visiting offices. Registration requires your Iqama number and a Saudi mobile number.

What is Absher?

Absher (أبشر — meaning "good news" in Arabic) is Saudi Arabia's unified e-government platform, managed by the Ministry of Interior (MOI). Launched in 2015 and massively expanded under Vision 2030, Absher has transformed how Saudi citizens and residents interact with government services. Instead of visiting multiple government offices, nearly all immigration and residency services can now be handled online.

For expats living in Saudi Arabia, Absher is not optional — it is essential. Your Iqama renewal, exit visa, driving license, and even your work permit are all managed through this single platform.

How to Register on Absher as an Expat

Registration is straightforward but requires a few things to be in place first. You will need your Iqama number, an active Saudi SIM card (the OTP is sent to a Saudi number), and basic personal details.

  1. Go to absher.sa or download the Absher Afrad app Available on iOS App Store and Google Play. "Absher Afrad" is for individuals — "Absher Amal" is for businesses.
  2. Select "Non-Saudi" and enter your Iqama number Your 10-digit Iqama number is printed on the front of your Iqama card.
  3. Enter your date of birth (Hijri format) The system uses Hijri (Islamic calendar) dates. Use an online Hijri converter if needed — your birth year in Hijri is approximately your Gregorian year minus 579.
  4. Enter your Saudi mobile number and verify the OTP This is why a Saudi SIM card is mandatory. The OTP expires in 3 minutes.
  5. Create your username and password Use a strong password. Note: Absher locks accounts after 5 failed login attempts.
  6. Log in and complete your profile Add your email address and review your registered details for accuracy.
⚠️ No Saudi SIM? You cannot register on Absher without a Saudi mobile number. If you just arrived, ask your employer to assist with Iqama registration before getting a local SIM, or visit a Jawazat (passport office) service center for assisted registration.

Step-by-Step: How to Renew Your Iqama on Absher

Iqama renewal is the most-used feature for expats. Here is the exact process:

  1. Log in to Absher and go to "My Services" Find the "Iqama Renewal" option under the Residency Services section.
  2. Check your Iqama expiry date and renewal eligibility You can renew up to 90 days before expiry. You cannot renew if your employer has "frozen" your Iqama or if there are unpaid government fines.
  3. Confirm your details are correct Sponsor name, profession, and personal details should match your Iqama. Report any errors to your employer's HR department before renewing.
  4. Pay the renewal fee (SAR 650) Accepted methods: Mada (Saudi debit card), Visa, Mastercard. Payment is processed instantly.
  5. Download your digital Iqama Your renewed Iqama is immediately valid digitally. The physical card is available for collection if needed, but the digital version is legally accepted.
💡 Pro Tip: Set a reminder 60 days before your Iqama expiry using our free Iqama Expiry Tracker. Late renewals attract SAR 500/month in fines. Renewing 2 months early gives you peace of mind and avoids the rush-period queues on the platform.

How to Apply for Exit/Re-entry Visa on Absher

Since the Kafala reforms of 2021, most private sector workers can now apply for their own exit and re-entry visas through Absher without needing employer approval. Here is how:

  • Log in → My Services → Exit & Re-entry Visa
  • Select Single Exit/Re-entry or Multiple Exit/Re-entry
  • Choose the validity period (3, 6, or 12 months for multiple exit)
  • Pay the applicable fee (see our Exit Visa Cost Calculator for current fees)
  • The visa is issued digitally and is immediately valid
⚠️ Exception — domestic workers: Domestic workers (housemaids, drivers, cooks) are still under the traditional Kafala system and generally require employer approval for exit visas. If your employer is blocking a legitimate exit visa, contact the HRSD hotline on 19911.

Absher Amal — For Business Owners

If you own a business or manage HR for a company in Saudi Arabia, Absher Amal is the business equivalent. It allows employers to manage employee visas, issue exit permissions, track workforce Iqama statuses, and submit requests to HRSD — all in one portal. It is separate from the personal Absher Afrad account.

Common Absher Problems and Solutions

My Absher account is locked — what do I do? ▼
Accounts lock after 5 failed login attempts. Click "Forgot Password" and you will receive an OTP to your registered Saudi mobile number to reset. If you no longer have access to the registered number, visit a Jawazat service centre or call MOI on 920004444 for assisted unlock.
My Iqama renewal fails with "employer blocked" error ▼
This means your employer or their PRO has placed a hold on your file. Common reasons: unpaid GOSI contributions, a pending labour complaint, or the employer is in a "frozen" status with HRSD. Contact your HR department. If unresolved, file a complaint at HRSD (hrsd.gov.sa) or call 19911.
I cannot register because I do not have a Saudi SIM yet ▼
Ask your employer's PRO (Public Relations Officer) to help register you using their verified mobile number temporarily, then update your number once you have a SIM. Alternatively, visit a Jawazat service centre for in-person registration assistance.
Can I check my Iqama expiry date without logging in? ▼
No — you need to log in to see your full details. However, our Iqama Expiry Tracker lets you calculate the days remaining if you know your Iqama issue and expiry dates printed on the card.
Is the Absher app available in English? ▼
Yes. The Absher Afrad app and web portal are available in both Arabic and English. The language can be changed from the top right corner of the screen. Note that some deep service pages may still appear in Arabic only.
