Frequently Asked Questions

What is GOSI and who pays it?+
GOSI (General Organization for Social Insurance) is Saudi Arabia's social security system. Saudi national employees contribute 9.75% of their basic salary + housing allowance (capped at SAR 45,000/month), while their employer contributes 11.75%. Expatriate employees do not contribute to GOSI occupational hazard only applies at 2% paid by employer.
Is there income tax on salaries in Saudi Arabia?+
No. Saudi Arabia does not impose personal income tax on employment salaries for either Saudi nationals or expatriate employees. The only mandatory deduction for Saudis is GOSI. Expats keep 100% of their gross salary as take-home pay.
Are allowances (housing, transport) subject to GOSI?+
GOSI contributions are calculated on basic salary plus housing allowance only, up to the SAR 45,000 ceiling. Transport allowances, food allowances, and other supplementary allowances are generally not included in the GOSI base, though the exact treatment can vary by employer. Always check your employment contract.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is there income tax on salaries in Saudi Arabia?

No. Saudi Arabia has no personal income tax for either Saudi nationals or expats. The only payroll deduction is GOSI for Saudis (9.75%) and Occupational Hazards for expats (paid by the employer, not deducted from your salary).

What is the formula for net salary in Saudi Arabia?

Net Salary = Gross Salary − GOSI (9.75% for Saudis on basic + housing, capped at SAR 45,000) − any voluntary deductions. For expats, Net = Gross (no statutory deductions).

Are housing and transport allowances taxable?

Saudi Arabia has no income tax, so allowances are not taxed. GOSI for Saudi nationals is calculated on basic salary + housing allowance only — transport and other allowances are excluded.
