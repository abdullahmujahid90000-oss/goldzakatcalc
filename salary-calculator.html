💡 Understanding Saudi Salary Components

Knowing how your salary is structured helps you plan finances and negotiate effectively:

🏠 Housing Allowance

~25%

Typically 25% of basic salary. Not subject to GOSI deductions. Can be paid as cash or company housing.

🚗 Transport Allowance

SAR 300-1K

Covers commuting costs. Usually SAR 300-1,000 monthly. Tax-free and not included in GOSI calculations.

📱 Other Allowances

Variable

Mobile, food, education, or performance bonuses. Discretionary benefits that increase take-home pay.

💰 GOSI Deduction

9.75%

For Saudis only: 9.75% of basic salary (capped at SAR 45,000). Builds pension and unemployment benefits.

Key Salary Principles:

📊 Basic vs Total Package

Always clarify if your offer states "basic salary" or "total package". A SAR 10,000 basic with allowances is very different from SAR 10,000 total package.

🔄 Allowances Are Tax-Free

Most allowances (housing, transport, mobile) are tax-free in Saudi Arabia. They increase your net take-home pay without affecting GOSI calculations.

📈 Negotiation Tips

Focus on increasing basic salary as it affects GOSI pension calculations and future raises. Allowances can be adjusted more easily by employers.

Important: Always get your salary breakdown in writing. Verify allowances are contractual (not discretionary) to ensure they count toward EOS benefits.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Saudi Salaries

How is net salary calculated in Saudi Arabia?
Net salary = Basic Salary + Housing Allowance + Transport + Other Allowances - GOSI Deductions (9.75% for Saudis) - Any other deductions. For expats, only 2% occupational hazard applies (paid by employer).
What percentage is deducted for GOSI in Saudi Arabia?
For Saudi nationals: Employee contributes 9.75% of basic salary (capped at SAR 45,000). For expatriates: No GOSI pension deduction; only 2% occupational hazard paid by employer.
Is housing allowance included in salary calculation?
Yes, housing allowance (typically 25% of basic salary) is added to your gross salary. However, GOSI deductions are calculated on basic salary only, not including housing or other allowances.
Do expats pay GOSI in Saudi Arabia?
Expatriates do not pay the pension portion of GOSI. Employers must pay 2% occupational hazard insurance for expat employees. Expats receive full salary minus any agreed deductions.
What is the average salary in Saudi Arabia 2026?
Average salaries vary by sector: IT professionals SAR 8,000-25,000, engineers SAR 10,000-30,000, teachers SAR 5,000-12,000, healthcare SAR 7,000-20,000. Use our calculator to estimate your specific take-home pay.
How do I calculate my salary after deductions?
Enter your basic salary, select nationality, add allowances (housing, transport), and our calculator automatically deducts GOSI (for Saudis) and shows your exact take-home pay in Saudi Riyals.
Is there income tax on salary in Saudi Arabia?
No personal income tax for employees in Saudi Arabia. However, Zakat (2.5%) applies to Saudi nationals and Muslim residents on savings/assets exceeding nisab. VAT (15%) applies to purchases, not salary.
What allowances are tax-free in Saudi Arabia?
Most allowances are tax-free: housing (25% of basic), transport, mobile, education, and performance bonuses. These are added to gross salary without deductions, increasing your net take-home pay.
