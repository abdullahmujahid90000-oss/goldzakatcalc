How the Salary Calculator Works

This calculator shows your Saudi Arabia take-home pay based on 2026 official rates. For Saudi nationals, GOSI deductions are applied at 9.75% on the GOSI base (basic salary + housing allowance), capped at SAR 45,000/month. There is no personal income tax in Saudi Arabia for anyone — neither nationals nor expats.

For expat employees, there are no mandatory salary deductions. Your gross salary equals your net salary. However, your employer pays 2% Occupational Hazards insurance on your behalf, which does not affect your take-home pay.

⚠️ Common mistakes: (1) Some payslips calculate GOSI on basic salary only — the correct base is basic + housing combined. (2) Transport allowance is NOT part of the GOSI base and should not be included.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there income tax in Saudi Arabia? ▼
No. Saudi Arabia has no personal income tax for individuals — neither Saudi nationals nor expatriates. Your only mandatory deduction as a Saudi national is GOSI (9.75%). Expats have zero deductions from their salary.
What is included in the GOSI base salary? ▼
The GOSI contribution base includes basic salary and housing allowance combined, up to a maximum of SAR 45,000/month. Transport allowance, phone allowance, food allowance and other cash benefits are excluded from the GOSI base.
What is the maximum GOSI deduction in 2026? ▼
The maximum GOSI deduction for a Saudi employee is SAR 45,000 × 9.75% = SAR 4,387.50 per month. Any salary above the SAR 45,000 cap is not subject to further GOSI contributions.
Do expats get GOSI deductions from salary? ▼
No. Expatriate employees pay zero GOSI from their salary. However, their employer must pay 2% Occupational Hazards insurance on their behalf, which has no impact on the employee's take-home pay.
How does EOS (End of Service) affect my calculations? ▼
End of Service (EOS) gratuity is separate from monthly salary deductions. It is a lump sum payment due when you leave your job. Use our EOS calculator to estimate your gratuity entitlement.
