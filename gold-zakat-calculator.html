📖 How to Use This Gold Zakat Calculator

Our free Gold Zakat Calculator helps Muslims in Saudi Arabia accurately calculate their Zakat obligation on gold holdings for 2026. Whether you own 24K investment gold, 22K jewelry, 21K bridal sets, or 18K accessories, this tool checks if you've reached the nisab threshold and calculates exactly 2.5% Zakat in Saudi Riyals.

Quick Answer: If you own 87.48 grams or more of pure 24K gold (or equivalent value in other karats) for one lunar year, you must pay 2.5% Zakat. For 2026, the gold nisab is approximately SAR 50,826.

1Calculate Your Total Gold Weight

Gather all your gold items and weigh them accurately using a digital scale. Include:

  • Jewelry: Necklaces, bracelets, rings, earrings, bangles
  • Gold coins: Saudi gold riyals, investment coins
  • Gold bars: Investment bars of any size
  • Stored gold: Gold in safety deposit boxes or at home

Important: Do NOT include gold-plated items or costume jewelry — only solid gold counts for Zakat.

2Identify Gold Karat (Purity)

Check the stamp or hallmark on your gold to determine its karat rating. Common karats in Saudi Arabia:

24 Karat

100%

Pure gold — investment bars, coins, some traditional jewelry

22 Karat

91.67%

Most common in Saudi jewelry shops — 22 parts gold, 2 parts alloy

21 Karat

87.5%

Popular for bridal sets and traditional designs

18 Karat

75%

Western-style jewelry — 18 parts gold, 6 parts alloy

3Enter Current Gold Price

The calculator pre-fills the current Saudi gold price (SAR 581/gram for 24K as of June 2026). You can update this if:

  • You purchased gold at a different rate and want historical calculation
  • Gold prices have changed significantly since you checked
  • You're calculating for a future date

4Check Nisab Status

The calculator instantly tells you if your gold meets or exceeds the nisab threshold:

  • Nisab Met: You own 87.48g+ of pure gold — Zakat is DUE
  • Nisab Not Met: You own less than 87.48g — No Zakat due (yet)

5Calculate & Pay Zakat

If you've met nisab and owned the gold for one lunar year (354 days), the calculator shows your exact Zakat obligation. Pay this amount to eligible recipients:

  • The poor and needy
  • Zakat collectors
  • Those whose hearts are to be reconciled
  • Those in bondage (slaves/captives)
  • Those in debt
  • In the cause of Allah
  • The wayfarer (traveler in need)

Pro Tip: Many Muslims pay Zakat during Ramadan for extra spiritual rewards. You can pay to registered Saudi charities like Ehsan or Birr.
