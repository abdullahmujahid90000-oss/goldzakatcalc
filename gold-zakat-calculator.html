⚖️ Islamic Ruling: Do You Pay Zakat on Jewelry?

This is a common question among Saudi residents. Here's what major Islamic scholars say:

Hanbali Madhab (Majority View in Saudi Arabia)

Zakat IS payable on ALL gold jewelry — whether worn regularly or kept in storage. The nisab is 87.48 grams of pure gold. This is the official position of Saudi Arabia's Permanent Committee for Islamic Research [[45]].

Important: Some scholars (primarily Hanafi school) exempt jewelry worn for personal use. However, since Saudi Arabia follows Hanbali fiqh, residents should pay Zakat on all gold holdings to be safe.

When Zakat Becomes Obligatory:

  1. Nisab Threshold: You own 87.48g+ of pure gold (or equivalent value)
  2. Hawl (Lunar Year): One Islamic year (354 days) has passed since you reached nisab
  3. Complete Ownership: The gold is fully yours (not borrowed or pledged)

📅 When to Pay Gold Zakat

Zakat is due after one complete lunar year (hawl) from the date your gold first reached the nisab threshold [[47]].

Example Timeline:

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Gold Zakat

What if my gold is below nisab?+
If your total pure gold is less than 87.48 grams (or worth less than SAR 50,826 in 2026), no Zakat is due. However, if you have other assets (cash, silver, business goods), combine their values — you may still owe Zakat if the total exceeds nisab [[53]].
Do I pay Zakat on gold I haven't owned for a full year?+
No. Zakat is only due after one complete lunar year (354 days) of ownership. If you bought gold 6 months ago, wait until the full year passes. However, you can pay voluntarily earlier if you wish [[47]].
Can I pay Zakat in cash instead of gold?+
Yes, absolutely. You pay the CASH VALUE of 2.5% of your gold. You don't give actual gold pieces. Use our calculator to get the SAR amount, then donate that cash to eligible recipients or Islamic charities [[38]].
What about gold-plated jewelry?+
Gold-plated items have negligible pure gold content and are typically not subject to Zakat. Only solid gold jewelry (18K, 21K, 22K, 24K) counts. If unsure, check the hallmark stamp inside the jewelry piece.
Should I use gold nisab or silver nisab?+
For gold holdings, use the gold nisab (87.48g). The silver nisab (612.36g ≈ SAR 6,742) is much lower and benefits those with modest assets. Some scholars allow using silver nisab to help more people qualify for Zakat, but for pure gold, stick to gold nisab [[51]][[60]].
Do I pay Zakat on gold coins (like Saudi riyal gold coins)?+
Yes. Gold coins are subject to Zakat if they meet nisab. Saudi gold riyal coins contain 87.48g pure gold (exactly one nisab). Calculate based on the pure gold content, not the face value [[45]].
What if I sold some gold before the year ended?+
If your remaining gold falls below nisab after selling, no Zakat is due for that year. If it stays above nisab, calculate Zakat on what you owned at year-end. Keep records of all gold transactions throughout the year.
