How to Calculate Gold Zakat

Step 1: Check if you own 85 grams or more of 24k gold (or equivalent). This is the Nisab (minimum threshold).

Step 2: If yes, and the gold has been in your possession for a full lunar year (Hawl), Zakat is due.

Step 3: Multiply the total value of your gold by 2.5% to get your Zakat amount.

Formula: Zakat = (Weight in grams × Purity × Gold price per gram) × 2.5%

Note: Worn jewelry used regularly is a matter of scholarly debate. Many scholars exempt it from Zakat. Unused jewelry stored as savings is generally subject to Zakat.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Nisab for gold Zakat in 2026?

85 grams of pure (24K) gold. If your total gold ownership (across all karats, after conversion to 24K equivalent) exceeds this Nisab and you have held it for one lunar (Hijri) year, Zakat is due at 2.5%.

Do I pay Zakat on 22K, 21K and 18K gold?

Yes. All wearable and stored gold is included. To check Nisab, convert each karat to its 24K-equivalent grams: 22K = 91.67%, 21K = 87.5%, 18K = 75%.

Do I pay Zakat on jewelry I wear daily?

Scholars differ. The Hanafi madhab requires Zakat on all gold including worn jewelry above Nisab. The Maliki and Shafi'i schools exempt jewelry in regular personal use. Consult your local scholar.

How is gold Zakat calculated?

Total weight (in grams, 24K-equivalent) × current SAR price per gram × 2.5%. Example: 100g × SAR 280/g × 2.5% = SAR 700 Zakat for the year.
