Zakat Nisab for Gold – What It Is & How to Check

Nisab is the minimum threshold of wealth a person must own for one lunar year before Zakat becomes obligatory. For gold, the classical Nisab is approximately 20 mithqal, which many scholars approximate to 85 grams.

How to check if you meet Nisab

  1. Weigh your total gold (excluding non-gold stones/parts).
  2. Ensure at least a lunar year (‘hawl’) has passed over this wealth.
  3. If weight ≥ ~85g (or equivalent in money), Zakat is due at 2.5%.

Quick example

If you own 100g of gold and the price per gram is SAR 250, the value is SAR 25,000. Zakat at 2.5% = SAR 625.

Common notes

  • Different madhhabs may treat personal jewelry differently; ask a scholar if unsure.
  • If combining multiple assets (cash, gold, trade goods) together meets Nisab, Zakat applies.
