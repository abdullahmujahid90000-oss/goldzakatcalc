How to Send Money to Bangladesh — Steps

  1. Have your beneficiary's details ready: full name, Bangladesh bank account or bKash/Nagad number, NID number, mobile number.
  2. Open your chosen provider's app (STC Pay, Al Rajhi mobile, Tahweel branch app).
  3. Choose Bangladesh corridor → select payout method (bank deposit, mobile wallet, cash pickup).
  4. Enter amount in SAR — confirm the BDT amount recipient will receive.
  5. Authorize with biometric, OTP, or mada card.
  6. Send transaction ID via WhatsApp to recipient — they can track it.

Required Documents (First-Time Senders)

  • Your valid Iqama or Saudi national ID.
  • Active Saudi mobile number registered to your Iqama.
  • SAMA-compliant proof of income for large transfers (above SAR 30,000/month total).
  • Beneficiary's NID (Bangladesh National ID) number.

bKash, Nagad, Rocket — Mobile Wallet Tips

Bangladesh's three biggest mobile wallets (bKash, Nagad, Rocket) are now supported by most Saudi remittance providers. Mobile wallet transfers are typically faster than bank deposits (instant vs same-day) and many providers waive the fee for mobile wallet payouts.

Important Compliance Notes

  • All Saudi remittance providers are licensed and supervised by Saudi Central Bank (SAMA).
  • Transfers above SAR 60,000/year may trigger source-of-funds checks (provide salary slip or contract).
  • Bangladesh Bank now offers an additional 2.5% government incentive on remittance to recipient accounts — check Bangladesh Bank's circulars for eligibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best way to send money from Saudi Arabia to Bangladesh in 2026?+
For SAR amounts below 1,000, STC Pay or Al Rajhi Tahweel offer the best combined fee + rate (instant delivery, low fee). For larger amounts SAR 1,000–5,000, Wise typically offers the best mid-market rate. For cash pickup in Bangladesh, Western Union and MoneyGram deliver within minutes.
What is the current SAR to BDT exchange rate?+
As of May 2026, 1 SAR is approximately 32.5–32.7 BDT depending on the provider. Mid-market is around 32.72 BDT but providers add a small spread (usually 0.5–1.5%) to cover their costs.
Can I send money to bKash, Nagad or Rocket?+
Yes. Most Saudi remittance providers (STC Pay, Al Rajhi, Enjaz, Tahweel) support direct deposit to bKash, Nagad and Rocket wallets. Mobile wallet transfers are usually instant and often have lower fees than bank deposits.
Are there any fees on receiving in Bangladesh?+
Bank deposits and mobile wallet transfers are typically fee-free for the recipient. Cash pickup at agents may charge a small handling fee (1–2% of amount), though most Saudi providers cover this for the sender.
Is the 2.5% government incentive automatic?+
Yes. Bangladesh Bank's 2.5% remittance incentive is applied automatically by the recipient bank when funds arrive — no separate application needed. Eligibility requires that the funds are received through a licensed channel (which all SAMA-regulated Saudi providers are).
