📖 How to Use This GOSI Calculator

Our free GOSI Calculator helps employees and employers in Saudi Arabia accurately calculate General Organization for Social Insurance contributions for 2026. Whether you're a Saudi national, expatriate worker, or GCC citizen, this tool uses official rates to determine exact contribution amounts in Saudi Riyals.

Quick Answer: For Saudi employees in 2026, the employee contributes 9.75% of basic salary and the employer contributes 11.75%, capped at SAR 45,000 monthly salary. For expatriates, only 2% occupational hazard insurance applies (paid by employer) [[29]][[36]].

What Each Component Covers:

🏛️ Pension Insurance (9% + 9%)

Builds retirement savings. Employees receive monthly pension after reaching retirement age (60 for men, 55 for women) or lump-sum payment if leaving Saudi Arabia permanently.

💼 SANED Unemployment Insurance (0.5% + 0.5%)

Provides 60% of average salary for up to 12 months if Saudi employee loses job involuntarily. Helps bridge gap between employment.

⚕️ Occupational Hazard (0.25% + 2%)

Covers work-related injuries, occupational diseases, and workplace accidents. Includes medical treatment, rehabilitation, and disability compensation.

Important: GOSI is calculated on basic salary only, capped at SAR 45,000. If basic salary is SAR 60,000, contributions are calculated on SAR 45,000 only [[36]][[51]].

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About GOSI

What happens if my salary exceeds SAR 45,000?
GOSI contributions are capped at SAR 45,000 monthly salary. If your basic salary is SAR 60,000, contributions are calculated only on SAR 45,000. You (employee) pay SAR 4,387.50 (9.75% of 45,000) and employer pays SAR 5,287.50 (11.75% of 45,000), regardless of actual salary above the cap [[36]].
Can expatriates opt into full GOSI coverage?
No, expatriates cannot enroll in the pension portion of GOSI. They only receive occupational hazard coverage (2% paid by employer). However, GCC nationals have the option to enroll in full GOSI coverage similar to Saudi nationals if they choose [[45]].
When can I withdraw my GOSI contributions?
Saudi nationals can withdraw GOSI as: (1) Monthly pension after retirement age, (2) Lump-sum if leaving Saudi Arabia permanently and not returning, (3) Disability benefits if permanently unable to work. Early withdrawal is generally not permitted except under specific circumstances [[47]].
Does GOSI cover part-time employees?
Yes, part-time employees must be registered for GOSI if they are Saudi nationals. Contributions are calculated proportionally based on actual salary. The same 9.75% (employee) and 11.75% (employer) rates apply regardless of full-time or part-time status.
What if my employer doesn't register me for GOSI?
This is illegal. Report non-compliance to GOSI via their hotline (8001243344) or through the GOSI website. Employers face SAR 10,000 fine per unregistered employee. You can also file a complaint with the Ministry of Human Resources (HRSD) via Qiwa platform [[51]].
How does SANED unemployment benefit work?
SANED pays 60% of average salary (calculated from last 12 months) for up to 12 months if you lose your job involuntarily. To qualify: must be Saudi national, registered for GOSI, lost job through no fault of your own, actively seeking employment. Not available for resignations or terminations with cause [[60]].
Are bonuses included in GOSI calculation?
No. GOSI is calculated on basic salary only. Performance bonuses, annual bonuses, commissions, overtime, housing allowance, transportation, and other allowances are excluded from GOSI calculations. Only the fixed basic salary component is considered [[37]].
Can I check my GOSI contributions online?
Yes. Register on the GOSI portal (www.gosi.gov.sa) using your Absher credentials. You can view contribution history, estimated pension, and download statements. Employers can access the employer portal to manage employee registrations and payments.
