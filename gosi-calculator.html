🏢 GOSI Calculator Saudi Arabia 2026

Calculate Saudi and expat GOSI contributions on basic + housing, with the SAR 45,000 cap. Saudi nationals pay 9.75% (employer 11.75%); expat employees pay 0% (employer pays 2%). Free, accurate, official rates.

Updated June 2026 • Saudi: 9.75% + 11.75% • Expat: 0% + 2% • Cap: SAR 45,000
In Saudi Arabia 2026, the GOSI base is basic salary plus housing allowance, capped at SAR 45,000. Saudi national employees pay 9.75% (max SAR 4,387.50) and their employer pays 11.75% (max SAR 5,287.50) — a combined 21.5%. Expat (non-Saudi) employees pay 0%; their employer pays only a 2% occupational-hazard contribution (max SAR 900), which is never deducted from the employee's salary. Note: under the GOSI scheme introduced in July 2024, contribution rates for newly registered Saudi entrants rise gradually over several years, so newer hires may see slightly higher rates.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the GOSI employee contribution rate in 2026?
The GOSI employee contribution rate for Saudi nationals is 9.75% of basic salary plus housing allowance, capped at SAR 45,000 per month. Expat (non-Saudi) employees do not pay any employee GOSI contribution.
What is the GOSI employer contribution rate?
For Saudi national employees, employers contribute 11.75% of basic salary plus housing allowance (capped at SAR 45,000). For expat employees, the employer pays only 2% occupational-hazard insurance on the same base.
Does GOSI apply to expatriate workers?
Expat (non-Saudi) employees do NOT pay an employee GOSI deduction. Only Saudi nationals pay the 9.75% employee contribution. For expats, the employer pays a 2% occupational-hazard contribution, which is not deducted from the employee's salary.
Is housing allowance included in GOSI calculation?
Yes. The GOSI base is basic salary PLUS housing allowance (capped at SAR 45,000). Transport, food, and other allowances are not included in the GOSI base.
What is the maximum GOSI deduction?
For a Saudi national, the maximum employee GOSI deduction is SAR 4,387.50 (9.75% of SAR 45,000) and the maximum employer contribution is SAR 5,287.50 (11.75% of SAR 45,000). For an expat employee there is no employee deduction; the employer's occupational-hazard maximum is SAR 900 (2% of SAR 45,000).
How to check GOSI contributions?
Employees can check their GOSI contributions through the Qiwa portal (qiwa.sa) or the GOSI mobile app.
Can I get a GOSI refund?
Expatriate workers may be eligible for a GOSI refund upon final exit from Saudi Arabia, provided they meet eligibility criteria.
What happens if my employer doesn't pay GOSI?
You can report non-compliance to the Ministry of Human Resources via the Qiwa platform or Absher.
