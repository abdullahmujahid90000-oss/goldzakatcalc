Frequently Asked Questions

What is the GOSI salary cap?+
GOSI contributions are calculated on a maximum monthly salary of SAR 45,000. If your combined basic + housing allowance exceeds SAR 45,000, only SAR 45,000 is used for the calculation. This means the maximum employee monthly deduction is SAR 4,387.50.
Do expat employees pay GOSI?+
Expatriate employees in Saudi Arabia do not pay employee GOSI contributions. Their employer is required to pay a 2% occupational hazard (work injury) insurance contribution on their behalf. Expats are not entitled to GOSI pension or unemployment benefits.
What does GOSI cover for Saudi employees?+
GOSI for Saudi nationals covers: retirement pension (annuities), work injury compensation (occupational hazard), and unemployment insurance (SANED - Sanid programme). Benefits depend on years of service and contribution history registered in the GOSI portal.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the GOSI rate in Saudi Arabia for 2026?

Saudi employees contribute 9.75% of (basic + housing) capped at SAR 45,000 per month. Employers contribute 11.75% for Saudis, plus 2% Occupational Hazards for all employees.

Do expats pay GOSI in Saudi Arabia?

Expats do not pay GOSI from their salary. Their employer pays only the 2% Occupational Hazards insurance on the expat's contributory wage. Expats receive no pension or savings benefit through GOSI.

Is housing allowance included in GOSI?

Yes — for Saudi nationals, GOSI is calculated on basic salary + housing allowance. Other allowances (transport, phone, commission) are excluded.

Is there a maximum GOSI wage cap?

Yes. The GOSI contributory wage is capped at SAR 45,000 per month for 2026. Salaries above this cap pay GOSI only on the first SAR 45,000.
