📖 How to Use This GOSI Calculator

Our free GOSI Calculator helps employees and employers in Saudi Arabia accurately calculate General Organization for Social Insurance contributions for 2026. Whether you're a Saudi national, expatriate worker, or GCC citizen, this tool uses official rates to determine exact contribution amounts in Saudi Riyals.

Quick Answer: For Saudi employees in 2026, the employee contributes 9.75% of basic salary and the employer contributes 11.75%, capped at SAR 45,000 monthly salary. For expatriates, only 2% occupational hazard insurance applies (paid by employer).

1Enter Employee Nationality

Select the correct nationality category. This is crucial because GOSI contributions vary significantly:

  • Saudi Nationals: Full GOSI coverage including pension, unemployment (SANED), and occupational hazard
  • Expatriates: Only occupational hazard coverage (2% paid by employer)
  • GCC Nationals: Optional GOSI enrollment with same rates as Saudis

2Input Basic Salary

Enter the employee's basic monthly salary in Saudi Riyals. Important: Only include basic salary — do NOT include housing allowance, transportation, mobile allowance, or other benefits. GOSI is calculated on basic salary only.

Note: The maximum salary for GOSI calculation is SAR 45,000. If basic salary exceeds this, contributions are capped at SAR 45,000.

3Review Contribution Breakdown

The calculator instantly shows:

  • Employee contribution: Amount deducted from salary monthly
  • Employer contribution: Amount company pays separately
  • Total GOSI: Combined monthly contribution
  • Annual cost: Total yearly GOSI expense

4Understand the Breakdown

For Saudi employees, the detailed breakdown shows:

  • Pension insurance: 9% employee + 9% employer
  • Unemployment (SANED): 0.5% employee + 0.5% employer
  • Occupational hazard: 0.25% employee + 2% employer

5Verify & Register

Employers must register employees with GOSI within 60 days of hiring. Verify calculations on the official GOSI portal using Absher credentials.

What Each Component Covers:

🏛️ Pension Insurance (9% + 9%)

Builds retirement savings. Employees receive monthly pension after reaching retirement age (60 for men, 55 for women) or lump-sum payment if leaving Saudi Arabia permanently.

💼 SANED Unemployment Insurance (0.5% + 0.5%)

Provides 60% of average salary for up to 12 months if Saudi employee loses job involuntarily. Helps bridge gap between employment.

⚕️ Occupational Hazard (0.25% + 2%)

Covers work-related injuries, occupational diseases, and workplace accidents. Includes medical treatment, rehabilitation, and disability compensation.

Important: GOSI is calculated on basic salary only, capped at SAR 45,000. If basic salary is SAR 60,000, contributions are calculated on SAR 45,000 only.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About GOSI

What is the GOSI contribution rate in Saudi Arabia 2026?
For Saudi nationals in 2026: Employee contributes 9.75% of basic salary (capped at SAR 45,000), Employer contributes 11.75% (9% pension + 2% unemployment + 0.75% occupational hazard). For expats: Only 2% occupational hazard applies, paid by employer.
What is the maximum GOSI salary cap in Saudi Arabia?
The GOSI salary cap is SAR 45,000 per month as of 2026. Even if your basic salary exceeds this amount, GOSI contributions are calculated only on SAR 45,000. This cap applies to both employee and employer contributions.
Do expatriates pay GOSI in Saudi Arabia?
Expatriates do not pay the pension portion of GOSI. However, employers must pay 2% occupational hazard insurance for expat employees. Expats are covered for work-related injuries and occupational diseases under this coverage.
How is GOSI calculated on salary?
GOSI is calculated on basic salary only (not including allowances). For Saudis: Employee pays 9.75% and employer pays 11.75% of basic salary up to SAR 45,000. Example: If basic salary is SAR 10,000, employee pays SAR 975 and employer pays SAR 1,175 monthly.
What does GOSI cover for employees?
GOSI provides: (1) Old-age pension after retirement age, (2) Disability benefits for work-related injuries, (3) Death benefits for beneficiaries, (4) Unemployment benefits (SANED) for up to 12 months, (5) Occupational hazard coverage for medical treatment and compensation.
Is GOSI mandatory for all employees in Saudi Arabia?
Yes, GOSI registration is mandatory for all Saudi employees and optional for GCC nationals. For expatriates, only occupational hazard coverage (2%) is mandatory. Employers must register employees within 60 days of hiring or face penalties.
What is the SANED unemployment insurance program?
SANED is part of GOSI that provides unemployment benefits to Saudi nationals who lose their jobs involuntarily. It pays 60% of average salary for up to 12 months. Both employee (0.5%) and employer (0.5%) contribute to SANED as part of the total GOSI contribution.
Can I check my GOSI contributions online?
Yes. Register on the GOSI portal (www.gosi.gov.sa) using your Absher credentials. You can view contribution history, estimated pension, and download statements. Employers can access the employer portal to manage employee registrations and payments.
