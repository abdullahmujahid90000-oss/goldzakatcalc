How to Send Money from Saudi Arabia Cheaply

Saudi Arabia is one of the world's largest remittance-sending countries, with millions of expatriates sending money home each month. Choosing the right service can save you hundreds of SAR per year.

What to Look For When Comparing Services

Exchange Rate Margin: The biggest hidden cost. A provider might charge zero fees but give you a worse exchange rate than the mid-market rate. Always check the total amount your recipient gets.

Transfer Fee: Flat fee charged per transaction. Can range from SAR 0 to SAR 50+. Some services waive fees for first-time users.

Transfer Speed: Urgent transfers cost more. Bank transfers can take 1–3 days. Instant services (STC Pay, Wise) can deliver in minutes.

Delivery Method: Bank deposit, cash pickup, mobile wallet. Cash pickup is faster but more expensive in most cases.

Best Services for Pakistani Expats

For SAR to PKR transfers, Al Rajhi Bank, STC Pay, and Wise consistently offer competitive rates. Al Rajhi has the widest branch network across Saudi Arabia making cash pickup easy.

Best Services for Indian Expats

For SAR to INR transfers, Wise offers some of the best exchange rates, while Al Rajhi and Western Union are good for cash pickup options. STC Pay is popular for instant mobile wallet delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to send money from Saudi Arabia? ▾
Generally, online transfer services like Wise and STC Pay offer the best combination of low fees and competitive exchange rates. Traditional banks (Al Rajhi, SNB) are convenient but often charge higher margins. Always compare before sending — rates vary by up to 5% between providers.
Is there a limit on how much I can send from Saudi Arabia? ▾
Saudi Arabia allows expatriates to remit their full salary abroad. There are no official caps for personal remittances for expats. However, individual transfer services have their own limits (typically SAR 10,000–50,000 per transaction). For large transfers, banks are generally best.
How long does an international transfer from Saudi Arabia take? ▾
Transfer times vary: STC Pay and Wise can be near-instant (minutes), Western Union offers same-day cash pickup, while bank transfers typically take 1–3 business days. During Saudi holidays (Eid, National Day), processing may be delayed.
Do I need an Iqama to send money internationally? ▾
Yes. All major money transfer services in Saudi Arabia require a valid Iqama or Saudi ID for KYC (Know Your Customer) compliance. Make sure your Iqama is valid before attempting large transfers.
