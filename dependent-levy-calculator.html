About the Dependent Levy

Saudi Arabia introduced the expatriate dependent levy in July 2017. The fee is SAR 400 per dependent per month.

Who is a dependent? Spouse, children (under 18 or in education), and parents sponsored by an expat worker.

Who pays? The fee is technically the responsibility of the expat sponsor (the worker). Some employers cover this cost — check your employment contract.

Payment: The fee is collected with the Iqama renewal fee through the government's online portal (Absher).

Exemptions: Saudi nationals are exempt. Some special visa categories may have different rules.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much is the dependent levy in Saudi Arabia for 2026?

SAR 400 per dependent per month. An expat with a spouse and two children pays 3 × 400 = SAR 1,200/month, or SAR 14,400/year. Newly arrived dependents are exempt for the first 90 days.

Who pays the dependent levy?

The expatriate (you), not the employer. It is collected when you renew your dependents' Iqamas or exit/re-entry visas through Absher.

Are any dependents exempt from the levy?

Yes. Premium Residency holders' dependents are exempt. Certain professionals on the Saudi skilled-occupations list and special-case categories may also be exempt — check with Jawazat.

How do I pay the dependent levy?

Pay through Absher → Services → Dependent Fees. Payment is required before any visa or Iqama service for the dependent (renewal, exit/re-entry, etc.).
