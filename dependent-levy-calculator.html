Understanding Dependent Levies in Saudi Arabia (2026 Complete Guide)

Expatriate residents in Saudi Arabia who sponsor family members (spouse, children, or eligible parents) must pay a monthly dependent levy as part of the Iqama renewal process. This government fee was introduced to regulate expatriate sponsorship and align with Vision 2030 workforce localization goals. Our free calculator helps you accurately estimate annual dependent costs using the official Jawazat rate for 2026.

💰 Current Dependent Levy Rate (Official Jawazat 2026)

The dependent levy is SAR 400 per month per dependent for all expatriate sponsors. This equals SAR 4,800 per year per dependent (400 × 12 months). The rate was phased in gradually (SAR 100 in 2017 → SAR 400 by July 2020) and has remained fixed since. Fees are paid annually upfront during Iqama renewal via Absher or Muqeem.

Important Clarification: Primary vs Dependent Fees

Many expats confuse two different government fees:

  • Dependent Levy: SAR 400/month per family member. Applies ONLY to sponsored dependents.
  • Primary Iqama Renewal Fee: SAR 650 (1st/2nd renewal) or SAR 1,000 (3rd+ renewal). Applies ONLY to the main employee's residency permit, NOT dependents.

This calculator computes ONLY dependent levy costs. Medical insurance (~SAR 600–1,200/year per dependent) is additional and varies by provider.

📋 Who Must Pay Dependent Levies?

  • Spouse (1 dependent)
  • Each child, regardless of age (1 dependent per child)
  • Eligible parents (if approved for sponsorship)

Important: There are no exemptions for children under 18, students, or unemployed dependents. The SAR 400/month levy applies to all sponsored family members.

Frequently Asked Questions About Dependent Levies

How much is the dependent levy in Saudi Arabia 2026?+

The official dependent levy is SAR 400 per month per dependent for all expatriate sponsors. This equals SAR 4,800 per year per dependent. Fees are paid annually upfront during Iqama renewal.

Do I pay an Iqama renewal fee for dependents?+

No. The SAR 650/1,000 Iqama renewal fee applies only to the primary employee's residency permit. Dependents only require the monthly levy (SAR 400/month) and mandatory medical insurance.

Is there a SAR 100 dependent levy rate?+

The SAR 100 rate was phased out in 2020. All expat dependents now pay SAR 400 per month regardless of sponsor salary tier.

Do I pay dependent levy for children?+

Yes, dependent levies apply to all dependents including children and spouses. There is no exemption for children under 18. The fee is SAR 400 per month per dependent.

Can I avoid dependent levy fees in Saudi Arabia?+

No, dependent levies are mandatory government fees for all expat dependents. They cannot be waived. Ensure you have sufficient funds for renewal to avoid fines and Iqama cancellation.
