Understanding Dependent Levies in Saudi Arabia (2026 Complete Guide)

Expatriates working in Saudi Arabia who sponsor family members must pay a monthly dependent levy as part of the Iqama renewal process. This mandatory government fee supports Saudi workforce development initiatives under Vision 2030 and regulates expatriate residency.

💰 Current Dependent Levy Rate (Official Jawazat 2026)

The dependent levy is SAR 400 per month per dependent for all expatriate workers sponsoring family members in Saudi Arabia [[13]][[15]][[21]]. This rate was phased in gradually:

  • July 2017: SAR 100 per month per dependent (initial rate)
  • July 2018: Increased to SAR 200 per month
  • July 2019: Increased to SAR 300 per month
  • July 2020–Present: Current rate of SAR 400 per month per dependent [[17]][[24]]

These fees are paid annually upfront during Iqama renewal. For example, sponsoring one dependent at SAR 400/month costs SAR 4,800 per year (400 × 12 months), plus the Iqama renewal fee of SAR 650 (for 1st or 2nd renewal) or SAR 1,000 (for 3rd+ renewals), and mandatory medical insurance.

📋 Who Must Pay Dependent Levies?

Any expatriate resident in Saudi Arabia who sponsors dependents must pay levies for:

  • Spouse (1 dependent)
  • Each child, regardless of age (1 dependent per child)
  • Eligible parents (if approved for sponsorship)

Important: There are no exemptions for children under 18, students, or unemployed dependents. The SAR 400/month levy applies to all sponsored family members.

⚠️ Consequences of Non-Payment

Failure to pay dependent levies on time can result in:

  • ❌ Denial of Iqama renewal for dependents
  • ❌ Cancellation of dependent residency permits
  • ❌ Fines of SAR 500 per dependent
  • ❌ Travel bans preventing dependents from exiting or re-entering Saudi Arabia

To avoid these penalties, always renew dependent Iqamas before expiry and ensure levy payments are processed through official channels: Absher, Muqeem, or authorized Saudi banks.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Dependent Levies

How much is the dependent levy in Saudi Arabia 2026? +

As of 2026, the dependent levy is SAR 400 per month per dependent for all expatriate workers sponsoring family members in Saudi Arabia. This rate was phased in gradually from SAR 100 in 2017 to SAR 400 by 2020 [[13]][[15]][[21]]. Fees are paid annually upfront during Iqama renewal.

What is the total cost for family Iqama renewal in Saudi Arabia? +

Family Iqama renewal includes: (1) Dependent levy: SAR 400 per month per dependent (paid annually = SAR 4,800/year), (2) Iqama renewal fee: SAR 650 (1st/2nd) or SAR 1,000 (3rd+), and (3) mandatory medical insurance: ~SAR 600-1,200/year. Example: 1 dependent = SAR 4,800 + SAR 650 = SAR 5,450 total (excluding insurance).

Do I pay dependent levy for children? +

Yes, dependent levies apply to all dependents including children and spouses. There is no exemption for children under 18. The fee is SAR 400 per month per dependent, paid annually during Iqama renewal.

Was there ever a SAR 100 dependent levy rate? +

Yes, the dependent levy was introduced in July 2017 at SAR 100 per month per dependent. It increased by SAR 100 each year: SAR 200 in 2018, SAR 300 in 2019, and reached the current rate of SAR 400 per month per dependent in July 2020 [[17]][[24]].

Can I avoid dependent levy fees in Saudi Arabia? +

No, dependent levies are mandatory government fees for all expatriate dependents in Saudi Arabia under Vision 2030 regulations. They cannot be waived, negotiated, or exempted. Ensure you have sufficient funds before renewal to avoid fines and Iqama cancellation.
