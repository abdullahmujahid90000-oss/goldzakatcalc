Calculate monthly fees for family sponsorship: SAR 400/month per dependent, exemptions, payment process

Updated June 2026 • Rate: SAR 400/month • No exemptions for most expats
In Saudi Arabia 2026, the dependent levy fee is SAR 400 per month for each family member (spouse, children under 18) on your sponsorship. There are no general exemptions for expatriates — all sponsored dependents incur this monthly fee.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the dependent levy fee in Saudi Arabia 2026?
The dependent levy fee in Saudi Arabia is SAR 400 per month per dependent family member (spouse, children under 18) as of 2026.
Who has to pay dependent levy?
All expatriate residents sponsoring family members on their iqama must pay the monthly dependent levy fee.
Are there any exemptions from dependent levy?
Yes, exemptions include: GCC citizens, diplomatic staff, and certain professions (check Ministry of Interior guidelines).
How often do I pay dependent levy?
Dependent levy is paid monthly, but you can pay for multiple months or a full year in advance.
What happens if I don't pay dependent levy?
Unpaid dependent levy will prevent iqama renewal for both you and your dependents, and may result in fines.
Can I sponsor my parents in Saudi Arabia?
Generally no, unless you meet specific criteria (e.g., high salary, specialized profession). Parents are not considered standard dependents.
How to pay dependent levy?
Pay through Absher portal, Al Rajhi Bank, SNB, or other authorized banks using your iqama number.
Is dependent levy the same as iqama renewal fee?
No, they are separate fees. Iqama renewal is SAR 650/year, while dependent levy is SAR 400/month per dependent.
Do children over 18 pay dependent levy?
Children over 18 must transfer to their own sponsorship or leave Saudi Arabia. They cannot remain as dependents.
Can I get a refund if I cancel sponsorship?
No, dependent levy fees are non-refundable once paid, even if you cancel sponsorship mid-month.
