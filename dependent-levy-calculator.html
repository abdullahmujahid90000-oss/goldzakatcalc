📖 How to Use This Dependent Levy Calculator

Our free Dependent Levy Calculator helps expatriates in Saudi Arabia accurately calculate family Iqama renewal costs for 2026. Whether you have a spouse, children, or parents as dependents, this tool uses official Jawazat rates to determine exact levy amounts, Iqama fees, and total renewal expenses in Saudi Riyals.

Quick Answer: Dependent levies are SAR 100 or 400 per month per dependent. Annual cost: SAR 1,200 or SAR 4,800 per dependent + SAR 650 Iqama fee. Total for 1 dependent (1 year): SAR 1,850 or SAR 5,450.

1Select Levy Rate

Choose the monthly levy rate based on your salary tier:

  • SAR 100/month: For workers with low/medium salaries (typically below SAR 3,000 basic)
  • SAR 400/month: For workers with high salaries (typically SAR 3,000+ basic)

Tip: Confirm your rate with your company PRO or Jawazat before renewal.

2Enter Number of Dependents

Count all dependents you plan to renew:

  • Spouse: 1 dependent
  • Children: 1 dependent per child
  • Parents: 1 dependent per parent (if eligible)

3Select Renewal Period

Most dependents are renewed for 1 year. Some professions allow 2-year renewals. Levies are paid upfront for the entire period.

4Enter Iqama Fee

Standard Iqama renewal fees:

  • First/Second renewal: SAR 650 per dependent
  • Third+ renewals: SAR 1,000 per dependent

5Calculate & Pay

Click "Calculate Total Fees" to see your total cost. Pay through:

  • Absher portal: absher.sa → Dependent Services
  • Muroor portal: muroor.gov.sa
  • Bank apps: Most Saudi banks support levy payments
  • SADAD: Pay at banks or ATMs

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Dependent Levies

How much is the dependent levy in Saudi Arabia 2026?
As of 2026, the dependent levy is SAR 100 per month per dependent for workers with low/medium salaries, and SAR 400 per month per dependent for workers with high salaries. These fees are mandatory for family Iqama renewals.
What is the total cost for family Iqama renewal in Saudi Arabia?
Family Iqama renewal includes: Dependent levy (SAR 100 or 400 per month), Iqama fee (SAR 650), and medical insurance. For example, 1 dependent for 1 year at SAR 100/month = SAR 1,200 + SAR 650 = SAR 1,850 total.
Do I pay dependent levy for children?
Yes, dependent levies apply to all dependents including children and spouses. There is no exemption for children under 18. The fee is SAR 100 or 400 per month per dependent based on your salary tier.
What salary determines SAR 100 vs SAR 400 dependent levy?
Workers with basic salary below SAR 3,000 typically pay SAR 100 per dependent. Workers with salary SAR 3,000 and above may pay SAR 400. Check with your PRO or Jawazat for your specific classification.
Can I avoid dependent levy fees in Saudi Arabia?
No, dependent levies are mandatory government fees for all expat dependents. They cannot be waived. Ensure you have sufficient funds for renewal to avoid fines and Iqama cancellation.
When is dependent levy payment due?
Dependent levies are paid annually during Iqama renewal. You pay 12 months of fees upfront (SAR 1,200 or SAR 4,800 per dependent) plus the Iqama renewal fee and medical insurance cost.
What happens if I don't pay dependent levy?
Failure to pay dependent levies results in: Iqama renewal denial, dependent Iqama cancellation, fines (SAR 500 per dependent), and potential travel bans for dependents. Renew immediately to avoid penalties.
How many dependents can an expat sponsor in Saudi Arabia?
Most expats can sponsor spouse and children. Some professions allow sponsoring parents. Check with Jawazat for specific profession-based sponsorship limits. Each dependent requires separate levy payment.
