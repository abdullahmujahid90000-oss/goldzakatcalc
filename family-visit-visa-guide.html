✈️ Saudi Family Visit Visa Guide 2026

Step-by-step: how to apply via ksavisa.sa, required documents, fees, health insurance & how to track your application — for all nationalities.

📅 Updated May 2026 💰 SAR 300 base fee ⏱️ 3–7 working days 🌐 ksavisa.sa + Absher ✅ All nationalities

📋 What Is a Saudi Family Visit Visa?

A family visit visa (تأشيرة زيارة عائلية) allows a Saudi resident (Iqama holder) to sponsor their immediate family members — wife, children, parents, in-laws — to visit Saudi Arabia for up to 90 days per entry. The sponsor must have a valid Iqama and apply online via ksavisa.sa (MOFA) using their Absher/Nafath account.

🔑 Key point: Unlike the family residence Iqama (which goes through Istiqdam office), a visit visa is 100% online — no office visit needed in Saudi Arabia. Your family applies for the physical visa sticker at their local Saudi embassy/consulate abroad.
⚠️ Visit visa ≠ Iqama: A visit visa is temporary. Your wife cannot convert a visit visa to an Iqama — she must exit and re-enter on the correct visa. Children under 18 can convert at Jawazat. → See full conversion guide

🗺️ Step-by-Step Application Process

PHASE 1 — Saudi Sponsor Preparation
1
NAFATH

Activate Nafath Identity Verification

Download the Nafath app (available iOS & Android) and link it to your Absher account. Nafath is Saudi Arabia's digital identity system — it generates a one-time verification code when you log in to government portals. Without Nafath, you cannot access ksavisa.sa as a sponsor.

Nafath setup: Open app → Verify with Absher credentials → Confirm with OTP on your registered mobile number. Takes 2–5 minutes.
2
ABSHER

Check Your Absher Account is Active

Log in to absher.sa → Individuals → My Services → Visa Services. Confirm your Iqama is valid (at least 3 months remaining is recommended before applying for a family visa). Your Iqama number is your sponsor ID.

PHASE 2 — Online Application (ksavisa.sa)
3
KSAVISA.SA

Log In to ksavisa.sa as Sponsor

Go to visa.mofa.gov.sa → Click "Sponsor Login" → Select "Absher / Nafath" login → Nafath app will send a push notification — approve it → You are now logged in as the Saudi sponsor.

4
KSAVISA.SA

Create New Visa Application

Click "New Application" → Select visa type: "Family Visit Visa" → Enter applicant details exactly as in their passport:

  • Full name (as in passport — romanised)
  • Passport number, issue date, expiry date
  • Date of birth, nationality, gender
  • Relationship to sponsor (wife / son / daughter / mother / father etc.)
5
KSAVISA.SA

Upload Required Documents

Scan and upload the documents listed below. Files must be PDF or JPG, each under 2MB. The system checks document quality — blurry uploads will be rejected.

6
KSAVISA.SA

Purchase Mandatory Health Insurance

ksavisa.sa will prompt you to buy health insurance before payment. Select an insurer from the approved list (Bupa, Tawuniya, MedGulf, AXA, etc.). Choose a plan — Class C is the minimum acceptable. Pay via credit/debit card or SADAD.

7
SADAD / BANK

Pay the Visa Fee

SAR 300 visa fee + SAR 27.6 e-services fee = SAR 327.6 per applicant. Pay via credit card, mada card, or SADAD bill payment. You will receive a payment confirmation email and SMS.

PHASE 3 — Embassy Appointment (Applicant's Country)
8
VFS GLOBAL / EMBASSY

Share Visa Approval with Family Abroad

Once approved (3–7 working days), download the visa approval reference number from ksavisa.sa and share it with your family member. They then:

  • Book appointment at nearest Saudi embassy or VFS Global office
  • Attend with original passport + printed visa approval + documents
  • Passport returned with visa sticker within 3–5 working days
VFS Global: Most countries now use VFS Global as the Saudi visa processing agent. Check vfsglobal.com/saudi-arabia for your country's nearest office.
9
ARRIVAL

Family Arrives in Saudi Arabia

Your family arrives at any Saudi airport or land border. Immigration officer scans visa sticker and stamps the passport with entry date. The 90-day clock starts from entry date — NOT visa issue date. Make note of the exit deadline.

📄 Required Documents

✅ Mandatory Documents

  • Sponsor's valid Iqama (copy)
  • Sponsor's valid passport (copy)
  • Applicant's passport (valid min. 6 months) — bio page scan
  • Recent passport-size photo (white background, 3.5×4.5cm)
  • Approved health insurance certificate
  • Proof of relationship: marriage certificate (for wife) or birth certificate (for children)

◇ Conditional Documents

  • Attested marriage certificate — required for wife's visa (notarised + apostille from home country)
  • Attested birth certificates — required for children
  • Previous Saudi visa (if applicable) — speeds up approval
  • Parents: sponsor's birth certificate proving relationship
  • Bank statement — sometimes requested for first-time applications
📌 Document Attestation Note: Marriage and birth certificates must be attested by the relevant ministry in your home country, then by the Saudi embassy there. Many nationalities (Pakistan, India, Philippines, Egypt) also need a Foreign Ministry stamp. Start this process 2–4 weeks before applying.

🏥 Mandatory Health Insurance

Health insurance is compulsory for all visit visa applicants entering Saudi Arabia. It must be purchased through the ksavisa.sa portal from an approved provider. The policy must cover the full duration of the visit.

Class C — Basic

SAR 140–300
Emergency care only. Minimum acceptable. Good for short visits (under 30 days).

Class B — Standard

SAR 350–600
Outpatient + inpatient cover. Recommended for longer stays or elderly visitors.

Class A — Comprehensive

SAR 700–1,200
Full cover incl. dental, specialist, maternity. For extended stays (60–90 days).
⚠️ Children under 12: Many insurers offer reduced rates for children. Select the family plan option when available — can save SAR 100–200 per child versus buying individual plans.

⚠️ Overstay Fines — Do NOT Ignore

Staying beyond the 90-day limit incurs daily fines AND a deportation order. These fines must be paid before departure and affect future visa eligibility.

Period OverstayedFine (SAR)Additional Consequence
1–30 daysSAR 500 flatWarning on record
31–90 daysSAR 1,000Travel ban until paid
91–180 daysSAR 2,000Possible deportation, 1-year entry ban
181+ daysSAR 5,000+Deportation + 3-year to lifetime ban
🚨 Exit before overstay: If your family member's visa is about to expire, you must either apply for an exit re-entry visa extension via Absher or ensure they depart on time. Do NOT wait until expiry day.

🔄 Extending a Visit Visa

You can extend a family visit visa via Absher before it expires. Extensions are not guaranteed and are subject to approval.

Extension path: absher.sa→Individuals→Services→Visa Services→Extend Visit Visa→Select dependent→Pay SAR 100–300 extension fee
Extensions are typically granted for 30 or 60 additional days. Maximum total stay per entry is usually 90 days for visit visas. If you need longer, the correct approach is a family residence Iqama — see our Istiqdam guide.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a Saudi family visit visa take to process?
Typically 3–7 working days after submitting via ksavisa.sa with all correct documents. During Hajj/Ramadan season it may take up to 2 weeks.
Can I apply for a visit visa without Nafath?
No. Nafath identity verification is mandatory to log in to ksavisa.sa as the Saudi sponsor. Your Absher account must be linked to Nafath first. Nafath setup takes 5 minutes via the mobile app.
Can my wife convert a visit visa to an Iqama?
No. A wife CANNOT convert a visit visa to a residence Iqama. She must exit Saudi Arabia and the sponsor applies for a family residence visa separately via Istiqdam. Children under 18 CAN convert at Jawazat (SAR 2,000 fee). → Full conversion guide
Can I sponsor visit visas for my parents?
Yes. An Iqama holder can sponsor visit visas for parents and in-laws, not just wife and children. Process is identical via ksavisa.sa. Proof of relationship (sponsor's birth certificate + parents' ID) is required.
What if my visa application is rejected?
Rejections often occur due to: incomplete documents, passport validity under 6 months, incorrect applicant details, or previous overstay history. You can reapply after fixing the issue. There is no appeals process — simply correct the error and submit a new application.
Can my family work on a visit visa?
Absolutely not. A visit visa does not permit employment of any kind. Working on a visit visa is illegal in Saudi Arabia and results in immediate deportation and a ban. For employment, a work visa (Iqama) must be applied separately.
What is the minimum Iqama validity needed to sponsor a visit visa?
Your Iqama must be valid for the duration of the visitor's stay. Most advisors recommend at least 3 months remaining on your Iqama before applying. If your Iqama expires while your family is in Saudi Arabia, renew it immediately — their stay becomes illegal too.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a Saudi family visit visa cost in 2026?

Single-entry family visit visa: ~SAR 300 (90-day stay). Multiple-entry: ~SAR 800 (1-year validity, max 90-day stays). Plus medical insurance (required, typically SAR 250–600).

Who can sponsor a family visit visa in Saudi Arabia?

Any expat with a valid Iqama earning above SAR 5,000/month can sponsor immediate family (spouse, parents, children, siblings). Some categories have additional salary/profession requirements.

How long does the family visit visa process take?

Typically 3–10 days through MOFA (Ministry of Foreign Affairs). After approval, your family applies at the Saudi consulate in their home country, which may take another 5–15 days.

Can a family visit visa be extended?

Yes. Family visit visas can be extended for an additional 90 days through Absher before expiry. Maximum total stay is generally 180 days per visit.
