📱 How to Recruit via Musaned — Step by Step

All domestic worker recruitment in Saudi Arabia must go through the official Musaned platform (منصة مساند), operated by the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Development (HRSD).

  1. Go to musaned.com.sa and log in with your Absher National ID or Iqama
  2. Select the worker type (housemaid, driver, nanny, etc.) and preferred nationality
  3. Choose a licensed recruitment agency from the Musaned-approved list
  4. Pay the SAR 649 Musaned platform fee (includes 15% VAT) by card or SADAD
  5. Pay the SAR 2,000 government visa fee through SADAD or your bank
  6. The recruitment agency handles the worker selection, medical fitness check, and flight
  7. Worker arrives in KSA — register their biometrics at the nearest Jawazat office
  8. Activate the Musaned Wage Protection System to pay salary electronically each month
✅ Tip: Only use Musaned-approved recruitment agencies. The platform lists all licensed agencies by nationality and city. Unlicensed recruitment is illegal and you lose all protections.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to hire a maid in Saudi Arabia in 2026?

The total one-time cost ranges from SAR 5,600 to SAR 22,000 depending on nationality. This includes: SAR 2,000 government visa fee + SAR 649 Musaned platform fee + agency fee (SAR 3,000–17,000) + flight + insurance. Ethiopian maids are the most affordable; Filipino maids are the most expensive.

What is the minimum salary for a domestic worker in Saudi Arabia?

There is no single government-mandated minimum wage for domestic workers, but Musaned-regulated agencies set minimum monthly salaries by nationality. Ethiopian: SAR 400–600, Bangladeshi: SAR 500–700, Filipino: SAR 800–1,200, Indonesian: SAR 700–1,000. Salaries must be paid via the Musaned WPS system from January 2026.

What is the Musaned platform fee in 2026?

The Musaned platform fee is USD 150 + 15% VAT = USD 172.50, which equals approximately SAR 647–649 at current exchange rates. This is paid once when submitting the recruitment request through the official Musaned portal.

Can I hire a domestic worker without Musaned?

No. All domestic worker recruitment in Saudi Arabia must go through the official Musaned platform. Recruiting through unlicensed channels is illegal and carries penalties under Saudi labour law. Always use an agency listed on musaned.com.sa.

What changed for domestic workers in January 2026?

From January 2026, all employers must pay domestic worker salaries electronically through the Musaned Wage Protection System (WPS). Paying salaries in cash is no longer permitted and can result in penalties. The WPS creates a digital payment trail protecting both employers and workers.

Does the domestic worker visa fee apply to people with disabilities?

Yes — people with disabilities (ذوي الإعاقة) are exempt from the SAR 2,000 government visa fee under Saudi social protection rules. They still pay the Musaned platform fee. Contact HRSD at hrsd.gov.sa for eligibility verification.
