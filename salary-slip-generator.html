What is a Salary Slip in Saudi Arabia?

A salary slip (also called a pay slip or pay stub) is an official document issued by an employer to an employee every month. It shows the full breakdown of earnings — basic salary, allowances, overtime — and all deductions such as GOSI contributions or advances. In Saudi Arabia, salary slips are essential documents for expats and nationals alike.

📌 When do you need a salary slip in KSA? Bank account opening, personal loans, car financing, apartment rentals, dependent levy proof, visa applications, and embassy documents all commonly require a recent salary slip.

What Should a KSA Salary Slip Include?

A proper Saudi Arabia salary slip should contain:

  • Company name and CR number — to identify the employer
  • Employee name, Iqama/ID number, job title
  • Pay period — the month and year of payment
  • Basic salary — the fixed monthly amount in your contract
  • Allowances — housing (بدل السكن), transport (بدل المواصلات), food, phone, etc.
  • Gross salary — total of basic + all allowances
  • GOSI deduction — 9.75% for Saudi nationals; 0% for expats
  • Other deductions — loan repayments, penalties if any
  • Net salary — the final take-home amount transferred to your account

GOSI Deductions on the Salary Slip

For Saudi national employees, GOSI (General Organization for Social Insurance) deducts 9.75% from the GOSI base, which is basic salary + housing allowance, capped at SAR 45,000/month. This appears as a deduction on the salary slip.

For expat employees, there is zero GOSI deduction from the employee's salary. The employer pays 2% separately as occupational hazard insurance — it does not appear as a deduction on the expat's pay slip.

⚠️ No income tax in Saudi Arabia. Neither Saudi nationals nor expats pay any personal income tax. Your salary slip will never show an income tax deduction.

Can a Salary Slip Be Used for a Bank Loan?

Yes — Saudi banks (Al Rajhi, Saudi National Bank, Alinma, Riyad Bank etc.) accept salary slips as proof of income for personal loans, car financing, and mortgages. Banks typically require the last 3 months of salary slips. The slip must show the company letterhead, employee details, and a clear breakdown of earnings and net pay.

What is the difference between gross salary and net salary?

Gross salary is the total of your basic salary plus all allowances (housing, transport, food, etc.) before any deductions. Net salary is the amount you actually receive after GOSI and other deductions are subtracted. For expats in KSA, gross and net are usually identical since there are no mandatory deductions.

Do expats get GOSI deducted from their salary?

No. Expat (non-Saudi) employees pay zero GOSI from their salary. Their gross salary equals their net take-home pay. Only Saudi nationals have the 9.75% GOSI deduction from their basic + housing allowance.

What is the housing allowance in Saudi Arabia?

Housing allowance (بدل السكن) is a monthly benefit paid by the employer to cover accommodation costs. By law, it must be at least 25% of the basic salary for Saudi nationals, though many employers pay more. For expats it is contractual. It is included in the GOSI contribution base for Saudi employees.
