Saudi Riyal Currency Converter — Real Rates, Real Transfer Costs

The Saudi Riyal (SAR) has been pegged to the US Dollar at 1 USD = 3.75 SAR since 1986, making it one of the most stable currencies in the world. That stability is exactly why every expat working in the Kingdom can predict next year's salary in their home currency almost to the rupee — but it is also why the "exchange rate" you actually pay when sending money is rarely the one you see on Google. This converter uses live mid-market rates so you can spot a fair quote in seconds, and the guide below explains how banks, exchange houses and remittance apps build the spread that costs you money.

Why the rate you get is never the rate you see

The number you see on Google, Reuters or our converter is the mid-market rate — the midpoint between the world's banks' buy and sell prices. No retail customer ever gets the mid-market rate. Banks and exchange houses charge a margin (called the "spread") to convert your money — typically 1% to 3% on major currencies and 3% to 7% on smaller currencies. On top of that, there may be a fixed fee per transaction. The combination of spread + fee is your real cost.

Worked example: You want to send SAR 5,000 to India. Mid-market rate is 1 SAR = 22.40 INR, so the "fair" amount is INR 112,000. Al Rajhi quotes you a rate of 22.10 INR (1.3% spread) plus a SAR 14 fee. Your real exchange rate is closer to 22.04 INR — and your recipient gets INR 110,200, not 112,000. That's INR 1,800 lost on a single transfer, or about SAR 80. Compare this to Wise at 22.36 INR (0.18% spread) with a SAR 6 fee — your recipient gets INR 111,800, saving you SAR 70 each time.

The SAR-USD peg and what it means for you

The Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) commits to defend the 3.75 SAR/USD peg by buying or selling US dollars whenever the market price moves more than 0.25% away from that level. In practice this means the SAR vs USD rate moves only between 3.745 and 3.755 — a band so narrow that for budgeting purposes you can treat them as fixed. Every other rate (SAR to PKR, INR, PHP, EGP, GBP, EUR) is computed indirectly: SAR → USD at 3.75 → then USD to the target currency at its open market rate. That's why a weak Pakistani Rupee or Indian Rupee makes your SAR salary worth more back home, while a strong Euro makes a European holiday more expensive — all moves are passed through USD.

When are exchange rates best for sending money home?

  • End of the trading week (Friday after 16:00 KSA): banks often offer slightly tighter spreads to clear positions before the weekend.
  • Mid-month, mid-week: avoid the last 3 days of the month — that's when expat-heavy currencies (PKR, INR, BDT, PHP) face peak remittance demand and rates widen.
  • During Ramadan and Eid: rates worsen by 0.5–1% due to surge demand. Send before the first week of Ramadan, or wait until 10 days after Eid for normalisation.
  • Watch for SBP / RBI rate decisions: the State Bank of Pakistan and Reserve Bank of India announcements often move the SAR-to-target rate by 1–2% within hours. Track via the central bank calendars.

Banks, exchange houses and apps — which is best for SAR transfers?

For corridors with high competition (SAR → PKR, INR, PHP, BDT), digital apps like Wise, Remitly, Western Union Digital and STC Pay Send consistently beat traditional bank branches and even Al Rajhi's Tahweel by 0.5–2% on rate. For low-volume corridors (SAR → EGP, EUR, GBP), bank wire transfers are often the only realistic option but you pay a steeper fee. Frequently asked questions

Is the Saudi Riyal pegged to gold or the US Dollar?

The US Dollar — fixed at 3.75 SAR/USD since 1986. The Riyal is not gold-backed in any direct sense. The peg is enforced by the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) which holds substantial USD reserves to buy or sell as needed.

Can I carry US Dollars cash and exchange them in Saudi Arabia?

Yes, but you must declare cash above SAR 60,000 equivalent at the airport. Cash exchange at airport kiosks gives the worst rates (1.5–3% spread). Better: exchange at a licensed money changer in major shopping malls, or deposit USD into a multi-currency Saudi bank account.

Does the SAR fluctuate against the Pakistani Rupee?

Yes — significantly. While SAR/USD is fixed, the PKR/USD rate moves daily. So SAR/PKR has roughly doubled over the past 5 years (from ~50 in 2021 to ~78 in 2026) because the PKR has weakened against the USD, not because the SAR has changed.

What is the cheapest way to convert SAR to USD for international travel?

A multi-currency debit card (Wise card, Revolut where available, or a Saudi bank card with low FX margin) used at point-of-sale abroad usually gives the best effective rate (0.5–1% above mid-market). Cash USD bought at a Saudi money changer typically costs 1.5–2.5% above mid-market.

Is there a tax on converting currency in Saudi Arabia?

No tax on the conversion itself. The fee or spread charged by your bank or money changer is the total cost. International transfers above SAR 60,000 from individuals may trigger source-of-funds reporting to SAMA but no tax.

Is SAR pegged to any currency?
