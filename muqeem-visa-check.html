📋 How to Check Muqeem Visa Validity — Step by Step (2026)

There are two ways to check your visa validity in Saudi Arabia — through the Muqeem portal or through Absher. Both are official government systems.

Method 1: Check via Muqeem Portal (muqeem.sa)

1
Open muqeem.sa in your browser

Visit muqeem.sa on your phone or computer. The site works in both Arabic and English — look for the language toggle at the top right.

2
Select "Visa Validity" / صلاحية التأشيرة

On the main menu, click "Visa Validity" (or "صلاحية التأشيرة" in Arabic). This is different from the Iqama renewal section.

3
Enter your Iqama number

Type your 10-digit Iqama number in the field. Then confirm with either your date of birth or passport number (depending on which you have ready).

4
Complete the CAPTCHA and submit

Enter the security code shown on screen, then press Submit / بحث. Results appear within seconds.

5
Read your visa status result

The result will show: visa type, expiry date, exit re-entry status, and whether your visa is currently active. Take a screenshot for your records.

Method 2: Check via Absher App

1
Open the Absher app

Download from App Store or Google Play, or visit absher.sa. Login with your National Access Number (رقم النفاذ الوطني) or Iqama number + OTP.

2
Go to "My Services" → "Residence"

Navigate to My Services → Residence Services → Iqama Details. Your visa validity, Iqama expiry, and sponsorship info are all shown here.

3
View exit re-entry status

Under "Exit and Re-entry Visa" in Absher, you can see if an exit visa is currently active and when it expires. This is separate from your Iqama validity.

✅ Pro tip: If you can access Absher, it gives more complete information than Muqeem. Muqeem is better for checking without an Absher account, using just your Iqama number and date of birth.

📖 What is Muqeem and Why Does It Matter?

Muqeem (مقيم) is the official Saudi government system for managing residence permits (Iqamas) and visa status for expatriates. It is operated by the Ministry of Interior (Jawazat) and is the authoritative source for all visa-related information in Saudi Arabia.

Every expatriate worker in Saudi Arabia has a visa record in Muqeem. Checking your Muqeem status tells you:

  • Whether your Iqama (residency permit) is valid or expired
  • The exact expiry date of your Iqama (in both Hijri and Gregorian calendar)
  • Your exit re-entry visa status — whether you can travel outside KSA and return
  • Your current sponsor (kafeel) information
  • Your visa type (work visa, family visa, etc.)
  • Whether there are any travel bans or outstanding government fines on your file

Muqeem vs Iqama — What's the Difference?

Many expats confuse Muqeem and Iqama. Here's the simple explanation:

What Does "Expired" Status Mean?

If your Muqeem check shows Expired, it means your Iqama has passed its expiry date and has not been renewed. This situation has serious consequences:

  • SAR 500 per month fine (charged to employer or employee)
  • Cannot exit Saudi Arabia until status is resolved
  • Cannot access many government and bank services
  • Risk of deportation if significantly overdue
⚠️ If your Iqama is expired: Contact your employer/HR immediately. The renewal must go through your sponsor (kafeel). If your employer is unresponsive, you can file a complaint at hrsd.gov.sa (Ministry of Human Resources).

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Muqeem and Absher for visa checks?

Muqeem (muqeem.sa) is a public-facing portal where you can check visa validity using just your Iqama number and date of birth — no account needed. Absher is the full government services app that requires login but gives more detailed information including exit visa status, sponsor details, and service requests. Use Muqeem for a quick check; use Absher for full management of your residency.

Can I check someone else's Iqama status on Muqeem?

You can only check a status using an Iqama number and the matching date of birth or passport number. Employers can check their employees' status through their Muqeem business account (منشآت). Family members may check dependent status if they have the required details.

My Muqeem status shows "expired" but I thought it was renewed — what do I do?

This usually means your employer has not yet processed the renewal in the Muqeem system, even if they paid the fee. Ask your HR/PRO for the renewal receipt and the new Iqama print. If the system isn't updated within a few days of renewal payment, visit the nearest Jawazat office with your paperwork.

How long does it take for a renewed Iqama to show in Muqeem?

After renewal payment via SADAD, the Muqeem system typically updates within 24–72 hours. Absher updates usually reflect faster (sometimes within hours). If it doesn't update within 3 business days, contact Jawazat.

Can I travel if my exit re-entry visa is expired but my Iqama is valid?

No. You need both a valid Iqama AND a valid exit re-entry visa to travel outside Saudi Arabia and return. If your exit re-entry visa has expired but your Iqama is valid, you must apply for a new exit re-entry visa through your employer before travelling.

What is the Muqeem portal in Arabic (مقيم)?

مقيم هو النظام الحكومي الرسمي لإدارة تصاريح الإقامة والتأشيرات للمقيمين الأجانب في المملكة العربية السعودية. يمكنك الوصول إليه عبر muqeem.sa للتحقق من صلاحية التأشيرة وبيانات الإقامة.

What phone number can I call for Muqeem or Jawazat help?

Call the Jawazat (Directorate General of Passports) on 920020820 for Iqama and residency inquiries. For Ministry of Human Resources (labour issues), call 19911. Both services have Arabic and English support.
