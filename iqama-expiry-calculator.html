📖 How to Use This Iqama Expiry Calculator

Our free Iqama Expiry Calculator helps expatriates in Saudi Arabia track their residency permit status for 2026. Whether your Iqama is valid, expiring soon, or already expired, this tool uses official Jawazat rates to determine renewal deadlines, fees, grace periods, and potential penalties in Saudi Riyals.

Quick Answer: Iqamas are typically valid for 1-2 years. Renewal must be initiated before expiry. Saudi Arabia provides a 3-day grace period after expiry. After that, daily fines apply: SAR 100/day (year 1), SAR 500/day (year 2).

1Enter Your Iqama Expiry Date

Input your Iqama expiry date from:

  • Physical Iqama card: Expiry date printed on front
  • Absher portal: absher.sa → Residency Services
  • Muqeem portal: muqeem.sa → Residency Inquiry
  • Employer/PRO: Ask your company's government relations officer

Tip: Set a phone reminder 30 days before expiry to start renewal process early.

2Select Renewal Count

Choose how many times you've renewed your Iqama — this affects the fee:

  • First Renewal: SAR 650 fee
  • Second Renewal: SAR 650 fee
  • Third+ Renewals: SAR 1,000 fee

3Add Dependents (Optional)

If renewing Iqamas for family members:

  • Enter number of dependents (spouse, children)
  • Each dependent may incur additional fees
  • Medical insurance required for all dependents

4Review Status & Deadlines

The calculator instantly shows:

  • Days until expiry: Time remaining on current Iqama
  • Status badge: Valid / Warning / Expired
  • Renewal fee: Exact cost based on renewal count
  • Grace period end: Last day to renew without penalty
  • Potential penalty: Daily fines if already expired

5Take Action Based on Status

Follow the recommended next steps:

  • Valid (30+ days): Plan renewal, gather documents
  • Warning (<30 days): Start renewal process immediately
  • Grace Period (0-3 days post-expiry): Renew TODAY to avoid fines
  • Expired (3+ days): Renew immediately + pay accumulated fines

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Iqama Expiry

How long is an Iqama valid in Saudi Arabia?
Iqamas are typically valid for 1-2 years depending on employment contract and sponsor preference. Most expats receive 1-year renewable Iqamas. Renewal must be initiated before expiry to avoid penalties.
What is the grace period for Iqama renewal in Saudi Arabia?
Saudi Arabia provides a 3-day grace period after Iqama expiry for renewal without penalty. After 3 days, daily fines apply: SAR 100 for first year of delay, SAR 500 for second year, and deportation risk after 3 years.
How much does Iqama renewal cost in 2026?
Iqama renewal fees in 2026: SAR 650 for first renewal, SAR 650 for second renewal, SAR 1,000 for third+ renewals. Additional fees may apply for dependents, profession changes, or medical insurance.
Can I renew my Iqama after expiry?
Yes, but penalties apply after the 3-day grace period. Fines accumulate daily: SAR 100/day (year 1), SAR 500/day (year 2). After 3 years of expiry, deportation proceedings may begin. Renew immediately to minimize penalties.
What documents are needed for Iqama renewal?
Required documents: Valid passport (6+ months), current Iqama, employer sponsorship letter, medical insurance proof, fingerprint verification (if required), and payment of renewal fees via Absher or Jawazat portal.
How do I check my Iqama expiry date?
Check via: (1) Physical Iqama card (expiry date printed), (2) Absher portal (absher.sa) under 'Residency Services', (3) Muqeem portal (muqeem.sa), or (4) Ask your employer/PRO to verify through Jawazat system.
What happens if my Iqama expires while I'm outside Saudi Arabia?
If Iqama expires while abroad, you cannot re-enter Saudi Arabia until renewed. Your sponsor must renew it from within KSA, then you can apply for a new entry visa. Plan renewals before travel to avoid complications.
Can I transfer sponsorship with an expired Iqama?
No, Iqama transfer (sponsorship change) requires a valid, unexpired Iqama. Renew your Iqama first through current sponsor, then initiate transfer process through Qiwa platform. Expired Iqamas block all transfer requests.
