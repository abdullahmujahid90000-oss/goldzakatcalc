Iqama Renewal Tips

Renewal deadline: Renew your Iqama before the expiry date to avoid fines. Start the renewal process at least 30 days before expiry.

Late renewal fines: Overstaying your Iqama expiry can result in fines of SAR 500–10,000 depending on duration.

How to renew: Your employer typically handles Iqama renewal. You can also check status via the Absher platform (absher.sa).

Hijri calendar: Saudi official dates use the Hijri (Islamic) calendar. The Hijri year is approximately 11 days shorter than the Gregorian year.

Iqama Renewal in Saudi Arabia 2026 — Everything Beyond the Expiry Date

Your Iqama is the single most important document you hold as an expat in Saudi Arabia. It's your identity, your work permit, your residence proof, your driving licence link, and your ticket back in if you leave. The tracker above tells you exactly how many days are left and whether you've crossed into the SAR 500/month penalty zone. The guide below covers the rules every Iqama holder should know — but rarely does — until something goes wrong.

When does the Iqama actually need to be renewed?

Your Iqama expiry date is shown on the front of the card. Renewal must be completed before the printed expiry date — not on it, not after. The renewal opens 90 days before expiry, and during that 90-day window the SAR 650 government fee plus the dependent levy (if applicable) can be paid through Sadad in Absher. If your employer is in good standing with WPS and GOSI, the renewal is instant. If they're in Yellow or Red zone in WPS, the renewal will be blocked until they clear up.

The cost ladder — what you pay at each stage

  • On-time renewal: SAR 650 government fee + dependent levy if applicable (SAR 400/month per family member) + employer's labour fees. Total typically SAR 650 + (dependents × SAR 4,800) — paid annually.
  • 1–30 days late: SAR 500 late penalty on top of the standard fee.
  • 31–90 days late: SAR 1,000 penalty, plus possible employer-side fine of SAR 5,000.
  • 90+ days late: Iqama automatically cancelled. You'd need an Iqama-replacement procedure costing SAR 1,000+ and potentially having to leave and re-enter on a new visa.

Who actually pays for your Iqama — you or your employer?

By Saudi Labour Law (Article 40 and Article 61), the employer must bear all government fees, labour permit fees, and Iqama issuance and renewal fees for the worker themselves. The employee is responsible only for: (1) the SAR 400/month dependent levy on family members the worker chose to bring (this is a personal cost regardless of contract), and (2) any late-renewal penalty caused by the employee's own delay in providing documents.

If your employer is deducting the SAR 650 from your salary, that's a labour-law violation — unless your contract explicitly converts that deduction into a "service" or "allowance" structure. Get a written breakdown and consult HRSD if in doubt.

Step-by-step Iqama renewal on Absher (2026 process)

  1. Check Iqama expiry in Absher → "Iqama Services" or directly through the tracker above.
  2. Confirm your employer (sponsor) is in Green zone for WPS and GOSI — your renewal will fail otherwise.
  3. Confirm you have a valid health-insurance policy for the upcoming year (mandatory).
  4. Confirm any active traffic fines (mukhalafat) are paid — open fines block the renewal.
  5. In Absher, go to Services → Iqama Renewal → review the displayed fee (SAR 650 + any dependent levy due).
  6. Pay via Sadad — the renewal is processed instantly upon successful payment.
  7. The new expiry date appears in Absher immediately. The physical Iqama card itself does not need replacement unless its physical condition is damaged or it expires the typical 5-year design cycle.

The new 5-year Iqama — what changed in 2025

Since late 2025, qualifying expats have been issued a new 5-year Muqeem card that replaces the old annual paper-renewal model with a 5-year validity. The annual renewal fee, dependent levy, and health insurance are still due each year — only the physical card lasts 5 years. Frequently asked questions

My Iqama has expired and I'm in Saudi Arabia — what now?

Don't leave the country and act fast. Contact your HR or sponsor immediately, pay the SAR 650 + SAR 500 penalty (first month), and submit the renewal. If less than 30 days have passed, you're in a recoverable position. Past 90 days, the Iqama is automatically cancelled and you'd need a full new-visa process from your home country.

My Iqama is about to expire and I'm traveling — should I renew first?

Always renew before traveling if expiry is within the next 90 days. Your Iqama must be valid for the entire duration of your exit-re-entry visa PLUS 90 days after planned return. If it'll expire while abroad, your re-entry will be blocked at the airport unless you (or your employer) renew remotely during your trip — which is possible but stressful.

Can my Iqama be renewed if my employer is in Red WPS zone?

No. Iqama renewal is automatically blocked for any sponsor in Red WPS zone (more than 30 days of late salary payments to ANY employee). The block only lifts when the employer resolves the WPS issue. If your employer cannot or will not fix it, your only path is an Iqama transfer to another sponsor — possible only if you've been with the current employer for at least one year and your contract permits.

What's the difference between Hijri and Gregorian Iqama expiry?

Until 2018, Iqamas expired on Hijri dates (which advance ~11 days each Gregorian year, so expiry "moved" annually). Since 2018, all new Iqamas have Gregorian expiry dates printed on the card. Older Iqamas may still show Hijri dates — Absher auto-converts and displays Gregorian for clarity.

Can I check Iqama expiry without logging into Absher?

Yes — through Muqeem (muqeem.sa) using just your Iqama number and date of birth. Also through the toll-free Jawazat hotline 989. The tracker on this page reads the expiry date from your input — for privacy reasons it does not connect to government systems directly.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Iqama renewal cost in 2026?

SAR 650 per year for all nationalities, plus a SAR 51.75 Absher processing fee. Employers are legally responsible for renewal costs, plus the monthly work-permit levy.

What is the penalty for a late Iqama renewal?

SAR 500 for a first-time late renewal, and SAR 1,000 for repeated late renewals. An expired Iqama can also result in a travel ban, frozen bank accounts and loss of Absher access.

How do I convert Hijri to Gregorian for my Iqama?

The calculator converts your Hijri Iqama expiry date to Gregorian automatically. Saudi Iqamas use the Hijri calendar by default and the Gregorian conversion shifts by ~11 days each year.

Can I renew my Iqama 3 months early?

Yes. You can renew your Iqama up to 180 days (about 6 months) before its expiry through Absher. Quarterly (3/6/9/12-month) renewals are also supported.
