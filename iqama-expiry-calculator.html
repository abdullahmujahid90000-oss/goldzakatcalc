How to Read Your Iqama Expiry Date

Your Iqama (إقامة) has two dates printed on it — a Hijri (Islamic calendar) date and a Gregorian date. For this calculator, use the Gregorian date shown on your card.

Where to Find Your Iqama Expiry Date

The expiry date is printed on the front of your Iqama card under "Date of Expiry" or "تاريخ الانتهاء". You can also check it on the Absher app or the Iqama inquiry service on the MOI website.

2026 Iqama Renewal Fees

Renewal fees depend on your visa type and whether your employer is compliant with Saudization (Nitaqat) requirements. Typical fees for 1-year renewal range from SAR 650 to SAR 2,000. Always verify current fees on absher.sa before payment.

When to Renew Your Iqama

You can renew your Iqama up to 3 months before the expiry date. Never let your Iqama expire — overstaying results in daily fines (SAR 100/day) and can affect your ability to exit Saudi Arabia.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if my Iqama expires? ▾
If your Iqama expires, you are considered an overstayer (mukhalif). Fines start at SAR 100 per day. You may be banned from renewing, face deportation proceedings, and have difficulty exiting the country. Renew at least 1 month before expiry.
Can I renew my Iqama without my employer? ▾
Your employer (kafeel) must initiate the Iqama renewal through Absher or Qiwa. If your employer refuses, you can file a complaint with the Ministry of Human Resources. Under new Saudization reforms, some employees can transfer employers without consent.
How much does Iqama renewal cost in 2026? ▾
Iqama renewal fees in 2026 vary from SAR 650 to SAR 2,000 for 1 year depending on your employment type, Nitaqat status, and visa category. Always check the latest fees on absher.sa as rates are updated periodically.
Can I check my Iqama expiry online? ▾
Yes. You can check your Iqama status on the Absher platform (absher.sa), the Iqama inquiry service on the MOI website (moi.gov.sa), or through the Tawakkalna app. You'll need your Iqama number and date of birth.
