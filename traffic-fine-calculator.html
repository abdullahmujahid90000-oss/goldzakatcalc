How to Use This Traffic Fine Calculator

Our free Traffic Fine Calculator helps drivers in Saudi Arabia accurately calculate penalties for 2026. Whether you received a Saher ticket, Muroor violation, or police fine, this tool uses official Saudi traffic laws to determine exact amounts, discounts, and deadlines in Saudi Riyals.

Quick Answer: Saudi traffic fines range from SAR 150 (minor violations) to SAR 6,000 (major violations like red light). A 50% discount is available if paid within 30 days of issuance.

1Select Violation Type

Choose the type of traffic violation from the dropdown. Common violations include:

  • Speeding: Exceeding speed limits (fines vary by excess speed)
  • Red Light: Running red traffic signals (SAR 3,000-6,000)
  • Seatbelt: Not wearing seatbelt (SAR 150)
  • Mobile Phone: Using phone while driving (SAR 500-900)

2Enter Fine Amount

The calculator auto-fills standard fine amounts based on violation type. You can adjust if your fine differs due to specific circumstances or location.

Tip: Check your SMS notification or Absher portal for the exact fine amount.

3Enter Days Since Violation

Input how many days have passed since the fine was issued. This determines:

  • 0-30 days: Eligible for 50% early payment discount
  • 31-60 days: Full fine amount due
  • 60+ days: Potential penalties and double fines

4Calculate & Pay

Click "Calculate Fine" to see your total payable amount. Pay through:

  • Absher portal: absher.sa → Traffic → Pay Fines
  • Muroor portal: muroor.gov.sa
  • Bank apps: Most Saudi banks support fine payments
  • SADAD: Pay at banks or ATMs

Key Traffic Rules:

📋 Saher System

Saudi Arabia's automated traffic enforcement system uses cameras to detect violations. Fines are issued electronically and sent via SMS. Check violations in Absher or Muroor portals.

💰 Payment Deadlines

Pay within 30 days for 50% discount. After 60 days, fines may double. Unpaid fines block vehicle registration renewal and may result in travel bans for expats.

⚖️ Dispute Process

Dispute fines through Absher or Muroor within 30 days. Provide evidence such as dashcam footage. Disputed fines are reviewed by traffic committees.

Important: Repeat violations result in higher fines, license suspension, and vehicle confiscation. Maintain clean driving record to avoid escalating penalties.

⚖️ Official Saudi Regulations & Sources

All traffic fine calculations follow official guidelines from Saudi government authorities:

Legal Requirements:

  • Fine Payment: Must be paid within 60 days to avoid doubling
  • Registration Block: Unpaid fines prevent vehicle registration renewal
  • Travel Ban: Expats with unpaid fines may face exit/re-entry block
  • Point System: Traffic violations add points to license; 18 points = suspension

  • Muroor (General Traffic Department) — Official traffic portal

Frequently Asked Questions About Traffic Fines

How much is a speeding fine in Saudi Arabia 2026?
Speeding fines range from SAR 150 to SAR 900 depending on excess speed: 15-20 km/h over (SAR 150), 21-30 km/h (SAR 300), 31-40 km/h (SAR 450), 41-50 km/h (SAR 600), Over 50 km/h (SAR 900).
What is the fine for running a red light in Saudi Arabia?
Running a red light carries a fine of SAR 3,000 to SAR 6,000. Repeat offenses may result in vehicle confiscation for 30 days and referral to traffic court.
How do I check my traffic fines in Saudi Arabia?
Check fines via: (1) Absher portal (absher.sa) under 'Traffic' services, (2) Muroor portal (muroor.gov.sa), (3) Saher SMS service, or (4) Ministry of Interior app. You need your Iqama/ID number.
Is there a discount for paying traffic fines early?
Yes, Saudi Arabia offers a 50% discount on traffic fines if paid within 30 days of issuance. Some fines may have different discount periods. Always check specific violation details in Absher.
What is the fine for not wearing a seatbelt in Saudi Arabia?
Not wearing a seatbelt carries a fine of SAR 150 for both driver and passengers. Children under 10 must use appropriate child seats or face additional penalties.
Can I dispute a Saher traffic fine?
Yes, you can dispute Saher fines through Absher or Muroor portals within 30 days of issuance. Provide evidence such as dashcam footage or witness statements. Disputed fines are reviewed by traffic committees.
What happens if I don't pay traffic fines in Saudi Arabia?
Unpaid fines accumulate with penalties. After 60 days, fines may double. Consequences include: vehicle registration renewal block, travel ban for expats, vehicle confiscation, and potential court referral.
What is the fine for using a mobile phone while driving?
Using a mobile phone while driving without hands-free equipment carries a fine of SAR 500 to SAR 900. Repeat offenses may result in higher fines and license suspension.
