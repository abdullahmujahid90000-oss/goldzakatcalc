How Saudi Traffic Fines Work in 2026

Saudi Arabia enforces traffic regulations through a comprehensive system of fines managed by the General Directorate of Traffic (Moroor). Fines are issued either by traffic officers on the road or automatically by radar and camera systems installed across highways, urban roads, and intersections throughout the Kingdom. When a violation is recorded, it is linked to the vehicle registration and owner's Absher profile.

All traffic fines in Saudi Arabia can be viewed and paid through the Absher platform (absher.sa), the Moroor portal (moroor.gov.sa), or the Absher mobile app. Unpaid fines accumulate and can block vehicle registration renewal (Istimara), Iqama renewal for expats, and in serious cases lead to vehicle impoundment or license suspension.

Most Common Violations & How to Avoid Them

Speeding is the most frequently issued fine in KSA. Saudi roads have speed limits clearly posted, typically 80–120 km/h on highways and 40–60 km/h in urban areas. Radar cameras are widespread and issue fines automatically. Even exceeding the limit by just 1–20 km/h results in a SAR 150 fine. Exceeding by more than 60 km/h can result in vehicle impoundment, a suspended license, and fines up to SAR 1,500.

Running a red light carries one of the heaviest fines — SAR 3,000 — and typically triggers an automatic license suspension. Red-light cameras are installed at major intersections in Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, and other cities. Using a mobile phone while driving (without a hands-free device) is a SAR 500 fine. Seatbelt violations are SAR 150 per person — both driver and each passenger must be buckled.

Driving without valid insurance is among the most serious document violations, carrying a SAR 5,000 fine and vehicle impoundment. Similarly, driving without a valid license is SAR 5,000. Always carry your driving license, Istimara (vehicle registration), and valid third-party insurance when driving in Saudi Arabia.

How to Pay Traffic Fines in Saudi Arabia

You can pay traffic fines through several official channels. The easiest is the Absher app — log in, go to "My Vehicles," and view any outstanding fines linked to your vehicle. Payment is accepted via SADAD, credit/debit card, or bank transfer. You can also pay at any Saudi Post office, SADAD ATM terminal, or through your bank's online portal using the SADAD bill number.

Fines must be paid before renewing your vehicle registration (Istimara). For expats, unpaid fines can also block Iqama renewal. It is important to check your fines regularly, especially if you recently changed vehicles or received a new license plate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I check my traffic fines without Absher?

Yes. Visit moroor.gov.sa and enter your vehicle plate number and ID/Iqama number to check outstanding fines. You can also call the traffic authority hotline at 993.

Do traffic fines expire in Saudi Arabia?

No. Traffic fines in Saudi Arabia do not expire. They remain on your record until paid and will block vehicle registration renewal and potentially Iqama renewal. There is no statute of limitations on unpaid fines.

What happens if I leave Saudi Arabia with unpaid fines as an expat?

Unpaid traffic fines are tied to your vehicle registration, not your Iqama exit. However, they will block your Iqama renewal if left unpaid. It is strongly recommended to settle all fines before your final exit from the Kingdom.

Is there a discount for paying fines early?

Saudi Arabia has occasionally offered traffic fine amnesty campaigns where fines were reduced by 50–75% for a limited period. Check the Moroor portal or Absher announcements for any active discount campaigns.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I pay a Saudi traffic fine?

Pay through Absher (web or app), the SADAD payment network, or directly at a Tahsil office. The fastest way is the Absher app under Services → Traffic Violations.

Is there a discount for paying traffic fines early?

Yes. Saudi Arabia offers a 25% discount on most traffic violations if paid within 30 days of issuance. After 30 days the full amount applies.

What happens if I don't pay a traffic fine?

Unpaid fines result in a travel ban (you can't exit Saudi Arabia), Iqama renewal blocks, and may double after a year. Severe violations can lead to vehicle impoundment.

How do I check Saudi traffic fines on my Iqama?

Log into Absher → Services → Traffic Violations. You'll see all unpaid violations linked to your Iqama or vehicle plate, including dates, locations and amounts.
