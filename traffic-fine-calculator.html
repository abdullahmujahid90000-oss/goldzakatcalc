⚫ Black Points System — Saudi Arabia 2026

Every traffic violation in Saudi Arabia carries black points assigned to your driving record. Points accumulate and trigger penalties. Points reset to zero on January 1st each year.

🔎 How to Check Your Traffic Fines (Absher)

You can check all outstanding traffic violations for yourself or your vehicle via the official Saudi government Absher portal — without visiting the traffic department.

  1. Log in to Absher at absher.sa using your National ID or Iqama number
  2. Tap "My Services" from the main dashboard
  3. Select "Inquiries" from the service categories
  4. Choose "Comprehensive Traffic Violation Inquiry"
  5. View all outstanding violations, fine amounts, and black points on record
  6. Pay directly via Absher app (supports 4 installments for fines under SAR 5,000)
💡 Alternative: You can also check and appeal fines via Najiz.sa or through any Saudi bank app using SADAD payment. Pay via STC Pay, Urpay, or Al Rajhi app.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fine for running a red light in Saudi Arabia 2026?

Since 2026, running a red light carries a fine of SAR 6,000 plus 8 black points and vehicle impoundment for 7–30 days. Crossing an orange (yellow) light also carries SAR 3,000–6,000. These fines were significantly increased under Vision 2030 road safety reforms.

How much is the fine for using a mobile phone while driving in KSA?

As of 2026, using a mobile phone while driving costs SAR 900 plus 4 black points. This fine was increased from the previous SAR 300–600 as part of road safety escalation.

How do I check traffic fines by Iqama number?

Log in to Absher using your Iqama number, go to My Services → Inquiries → Comprehensive Traffic Violation Inquiry. You can view and pay all outstanding fines directly. Alternatively, visit Najiz.sa to check and appeal violations.

Can I get a discount on Saudi traffic fines?

Yes. Pay within 15 days of receiving the notification to receive a 25% reduction on the minimum applicable fine. You can also request a payment extension up to 90 days within the first 30 days. Discounts are not available after 45 days from the violation date.

Are traffic fines linked to the car or the driver in Saudi Arabia?

Most fines (especially Saher camera violations) are linked to the vehicle registration. However, violations like driving without a license are linked to the driver. If you sell a car with outstanding fines, clear them first — otherwise they remain on the vehicle record.

What happens if I have 24 black points?

At 24 black points your driving license is suspended for 30 days. At 36 points, suspension extends to 3 months plus a mandatory training course. At 48+ points, your license may be permanently revoked. Points reset to zero on January 1st each year.

Can expats with Iqama get Saudi traffic fines?

Yes. All residents — Saudi nationals and expatriates — are subject to the same traffic laws and fine schedule. Fines appear on your Iqama record and must be paid before you can renew your Iqama or exit the country.
