📊 Required Elements for Saudi Experience Letters

According to Ministry of Human Resources guidelines, a valid experience letter in Saudi Arabia must include:

  • Company Letterhead: Official company logo, name, address, and contact information
  • Employee Details: Full name, Iqama number, nationality, and passport number
  • Employment Information: Position, department, employment dates, and job responsibilities
  • Salary Details: Basic salary and allowances (optional but recommended)
  • Company Authorization: Signature of authorized signatory, company stamp, and date
  • Language: Arabic or bilingual format for official acceptance

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is an experience letter in Saudi Arabia?
An experience letter is an official document from your employer confirming your employment period, position, and responsibilities in Saudi Arabia. It's required for new job applications, visa processing, and professional licensing.
Is an experience letter mandatory in KSA?
While not legally mandatory, most employers and government agencies require an experience letter for new job applications, visa transfers, and professional certifications in Saudi Arabia.
What should be included in a Saudi experience letter?
A Saudi experience letter should include: company letterhead, employee full name, Iqama number, position, employment dates, job responsibilities, salary details (optional), and company stamp/signature.
Can I generate my own experience letter?
You can create a draft using our generator, but it must be printed on official company letterhead and signed/stamped by your employer to be valid.
Is Arabic required for experience letters?
For official purposes in Saudi Arabia, experience letters should be in Arabic or bilingual (Arabic/English). English-only letters may not be accepted by government agencies.
How long is an experience letter valid?
Experience letters don't have an official expiry date, but most employers prefer letters issued within the last 3–6 months.
What if my employer refuses to provide an experience letter?
Under Saudi Labour Law, employers should provide experience letters upon request. If refused, you can file a complaint with the Ministry of Human Resources through Qiwa or Absher.
Can experience letters include salary information?
Yes, salary information can be included but is optional. Some employers prefer not to disclose salary details in experience letters.
