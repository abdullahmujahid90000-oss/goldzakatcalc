About Saudi Arabia VAT

Saudi Arabia introduced VAT at 5% in January 2018, then increased it to 15% in July 2020 as part of economic reform measures. It applies to most goods and services.

Exempt from VAT

  • Residential property rental
  • Life insurance
  • Financial services
  • Some basic food items
  • Qualifying medicines and medical devices

Add VAT: Total = Price × 1.15

Remove VAT: Net = Total ÷ 1.15

VAT amount: Total - Net price

Understanding VAT in Saudi Arabia

Value Added Tax (VAT) in Saudi Arabia is a consumption tax applied at each stage of the supply chain. It was first introduced on 1 January 2018 at a rate of 5% as part of the GCC-wide VAT framework. The rate was then tripled to 15% effective 1 July 2020 as part of the government's COVID-19 economic recovery measures under Vision 2030. Saudi Arabia is administered by the Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA), formerly known as GAZT.

VAT applies to nearly all goods and services sold or consumed within the Kingdom. Businesses with taxable supplies exceeding SAR 375,000 per year are required to register for VAT. Businesses with supplies between SAR 187,500 and SAR 375,000 may register voluntarily. Non-registered businesses must not charge VAT to customers.

When Do You Pay VAT as a Consumer?

As an individual consumer in Saudi Arabia, you pay VAT automatically on most purchases — it is already included in the price shown at shops, restaurants, and online stores. When a price tag says "VAT inclusive," the 15% tax is already built into the total. When a quote or invoice shows "before VAT" or "exclusive of VAT," you need to add 15% to calculate what you will actually pay.

Common purchases where VAT applies at 15%: electronics and appliances, clothing and footwear, restaurant meals and cafes, hotel stays, vehicle purchases and repairs, fuel, telecommunications, and most professional services. VAT receipts must show the supplier's VAT registration number, the tax amount charged, and the total price inclusive of VAT.

VAT for Businesses and Employers in KSA

Saudi businesses registered for VAT must file periodic VAT returns with ZATCA — either monthly (if annual taxable turnover exceeds SAR 40 million) or quarterly (for smaller businesses). VAT-registered businesses collect VAT from their customers (output VAT) and can recover VAT they paid on business purchases (input VAT). The difference is remitted to ZATCA. Failure to register, file, or pay VAT on time results in penalties of up to 100% of the tax due.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is VAT charged on rent in Saudi Arabia?

Residential property rental is exempt from VAT in Saudi Arabia. However, commercial property rentals are subject to the standard 15% VAT rate. If you are renting an apartment or villa as a home, no VAT applies to the rent.

Are salaries subject to VAT?

No. Employment salaries and wages are not subject to VAT in Saudi Arabia. VAT applies to the sale of goods and services, not to employment income. There is also no personal income tax on salaries in the Kingdom.

Can tourists get a VAT refund in Saudi Arabia?

Yes. Saudi Arabia operates a tourist VAT refund scheme at major airports. Eligible tourists can claim refunds on VAT paid on qualifying purchases made during their visit. Look for the VAT refund kiosk at the airport before departure. Minimum purchase thresholds and conditions apply.

Will VAT increase beyond 15% in Saudi Arabia?

As of 2026, the VAT rate remains at 15% with no announced plans to change it. The government has stated the increase to 15% was a temporary measure for COVID-19 recovery, though no reduction back to 5% has been confirmed. Always check ZATCA's official website for the latest rate information.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the VAT rate in Saudi Arabia in 2026?

15%, effective since 1 July 2020. Some categories are zero-rated (exports) or exempt (residential rent, healthcare, education, financial services).

How do I add VAT to a price?

Multiply the net price by 1.15. Example: SAR 100 × 1.15 = SAR 115 VAT-inclusive. To extract VAT from a gross price, divide by 1.15 to get net, and subtract.

Who must charge VAT in Saudi Arabia?

Any business with annual taxable turnover above SAR 375,000 must register and charge VAT via ZATCA. Businesses with turnover between SAR 187,500 and SAR 375,000 may register voluntarily.

Is VAT charged on rent in Saudi Arabia?

Residential rent is exempt from VAT. Commercial rent is subject to 15% VAT.
