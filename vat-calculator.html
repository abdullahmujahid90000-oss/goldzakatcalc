📊 Worked Examples: VAT Calculations

Let's walk through real calculations for different scenarios common in Saudi Arabia:

Example 1: Adding 15% VAT to a Product Price

Scenario: You're selling a product for SAR 1,000 (excluding VAT). What's the price including VAT?

Step-by-Step Calculation:

Component Calculation Amount (SAR)
Original Price (excl. VAT) - 1,000.00
VAT @ 15% 1,000 × 0.15 150.00
Total Price (incl. VAT) 1,000 + 150 1,150.00
Result: The product price including 15% VAT is SAR 1,150. You collect SAR 150 as VAT from the customer.

Example 2: Removing 15% VAT from Total Price

Scenario: You paid SAR 2,300 for a service (including VAT). What was the original price before VAT?

Step-by-Step Calculation:

Component Calculation Amount (SAR)
Total Price (incl. VAT) - 2,300.00
Original Price (excl. VAT) 2,300 ÷ 1.15 2,000.00
VAT Amount 2,300 - 2,000 300.00
VAT Paid 15% of 2,000 300.00
Result: The original price before VAT was SAR 2,000. You paid SAR 300 as VAT (15% of SAR 2,000).

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About VAT in Saudi Arabia

What is the VAT rate in Saudi Arabia 2026?
The VAT rate in Saudi Arabia is 15% as of 2026. This was increased from 5% to 15% in July 2020 and remains the standard rate for most goods and services.
How do I calculate 15% VAT in Saudi Arabia?
To add 15% VAT: Multiply the price by 1.15. To remove 15% VAT: Divide the total by 1.15. For example, SAR 100 + 15% VAT = SAR 115. SAR 115 - 15% VAT = SAR 100.
What items are exempt from VAT in Saudi Arabia?
Exempt items include: residential rent, financial services, local passenger transport, and certain healthcare and education services. Zero-rated items include exports, international transport, and certain medical equipment.
Do I need to register for VAT in Saudi Arabia?
Mandatory registration if annual taxable supplies exceed SAR 375,000. Voluntary registration if supplies exceed SAR 187,500. Foreign businesses may need to register regardless of threshold.
How often do I file VAT returns in Saudi Arabia?
VAT returns are filed quarterly for most businesses. Monthly filing required if annual turnover exceeds SAR 40 million. Returns due within one month after the tax period ends.
Can I claim input VAT on business purchases?
Yes, VAT-registered businesses can claim input VAT on business-related purchases. Keep valid tax invoices and ensure purchases are for taxable business activities.
What is the difference between VAT inclusive and exclusive?
VAT exclusive means price before tax. VAT inclusive means price includes 15% VAT. To convert: Exclusive × 1.15 = Inclusive. Inclusive ÷ 1.15 = Exclusive.
Where do I register for VAT in Saudi Arabia?
Register for VAT through the ZATCA (Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority) portal at zatca.gov.sa. You'll need your CR number, contact details, and business information.
