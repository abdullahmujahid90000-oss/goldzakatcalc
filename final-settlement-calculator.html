Key Labor Law Provisions:

📜 Article 86: Payment Timeline

Employer must pay all dues within 10 days of employment termination. Delays allow employee to claim additional compensation equal to the delayed amount.

📜 Article 77: Vacation Encashment

Unused annual leave must be encashed at basic salary rate. Maximum encashment usually capped at current year + 1 previous year unless contract states otherwise.

📜 Article 75: Notice Period

If contract requires notice and it's not served, the non-compliant party compensates the other for the notice period duration.

Important: Always request a detailed settlement statement from HR. Verify each component matches Labor Law calculations before signing release documents.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Final Settlement

What is included in final settlement in Saudi Arabia?
Final settlement typically includes: End of Service (EOS) gratuity, encashment of unused annual leave, unpaid salary for worked days, notice period compensation (if applicable), and return ticket refund. Deductions may apply for loans, advances, or damages.
How is vacation encashment calculated in Saudi Arabia?
Vacation encashment is calculated as: (Basic Salary ÷ 30) × Number of unused vacation days. For example, if basic salary is SAR 6,000 and you have 10 unused days, encashment = (6,000 ÷ 30) × 10 = SAR 2,000.
When must final settlement be paid in Saudi Arabia?
According to Saudi Labor Law, final settlement must be paid within 10 days of employment termination. Delays may result in additional compensation or penalties enforced by HRSD.
Do expatriates receive final settlement in Saudi Arabia?
Yes, expatriates are entitled to full final settlement under Saudi Labor Law, including EOS gratuity, vacation pay, unpaid salary, and return ticket, provided they meet service requirements.
Can employer deduct from final settlement?
Employers may only deduct: (1) Outstanding loans/advances with employee consent, (2) Court-ordered deductions, (3) Damages proven and approved by HRSD. Arbitrary deductions are illegal.
What happens if employer delays final settlement?
If final settlement is delayed beyond 10 days, employees can file a complaint with HRSD. The employer may be ordered to pay additional compensation equal to the delayed amount or face fines.
Is notice period pay included in final settlement?
Yes, if the employee did not serve the required notice period, the employer may deduct notice period pay. Conversely, if the employer terminates without notice, they must pay notice period compensation.
How do I calculate my final settlement manually?
Add: EOS gratuity + (Basic/30 × unused leave days) + unpaid salary days + notice period (if owed). Subtract: loans, advances, or approved deductions. Use our calculator for accurate results.
