Wise — Best Overall for SAR to PKR

Wise (formerly TransferWise) uses the mid-market exchange rate with a transparent percentage fee. For regular workers sending SAR 1,000–5,000 monthly, Wise typically delivers the most PKR per riyal. The app is available in Saudi Arabia, supports Saudi bank accounts and credit cards, and transfers usually arrive within 24 hours.

How to use Wise from Saudi Arabia

  1. Download the Wise app or visit wise.com
  2. Create an account with your Iqama or Saudi ID
  3. Enter the amount in SAR and select PKR as destination
  4. Add your recipient's Pakistani bank account (Meezan, HBL, UBL, etc.)
  5. Pay from your Saudi bank account or debit card

Al Rajhi Bank — Best for Cash Pickup

Al Rajhi Bank has one of the widest pickup networks in Pakistan, with agents in every city and most towns. If your family does not have a bank account, Al Rajhi is still a reliable choice. However, the exchange rate is typically 2–4% below the mid-market rate, making it more expensive for large transfers.

STC Pay — Best for Speed

STC Pay can send money to Pakistan within minutes for STC subscribers. The rates are competitive and improving. Available directly from your phone — no branch visit needed. Best used when speed matters more than maximizing the exchange rate.

Tips to Save More When Sending to Pakistan

  • Send larger amounts less frequently — flat fees eat less into bigger transfers
  • Avoid weekends — exchange rates can widen on Fridays/Saturdays
  • Use bank accounts, not cash pickup — bank-to-bank rates are usually better
  • Check for new-user promotions — Wise and Remitly often offer first-transfer fee waivers
  • Compare before every transfer — rates change daily

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best app to send money from Saudi Arabia to Pakistan in 2026? ▼
Wise and STC Pay offer the best SAR to PKR exchange rates in 2026. Wise is best for maximum PKR per riyal. Al Rajhi is best for cash pickup. Remitly is best for first-time users due to promotions.
Is there a limit on how much I can send from Saudi Arabia to Pakistan? ▼
Saudi Arabia does not impose limits on personal remittances for residents with a valid Iqama. However, individual services have daily/monthly limits: Wise (up to SAR 50,000 per day), Al Rajhi Bank (SAR 100,000 per transaction via app). For large amounts, bank wire is most reliable.
Do I need an Iqama to send money from Saudi Arabia? ▼
Yes. All official money transfer services in Saudi Arabia require valid identification — your Iqama (residency permit) or Saudi national ID. You cannot legally send money through official channels without a valid Iqama.
