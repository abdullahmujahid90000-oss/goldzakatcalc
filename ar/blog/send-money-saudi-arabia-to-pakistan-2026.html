Why the SAR to PKR Corridor Matters

Over 2.6 million Pakistanis live and work in Saudi Arabia, making it the single largest source country of remittances to Pakistan. The Saudi Arabia to Pakistan corridor handles billions of SAR annually — choosing the right service can save you thousands of rupees every year.

The SAR is pegged to the USD at 3.75, so the SAR is very stable. The PKR however fluctuates significantly against the dollar, meaning exchange rates change frequently and the gap between services matters enormously.

Wise — Best Rate for SAR to PKR

Wise (formerly TransferWise) consistently delivers the most PKR per riyal. They use the mid-market exchange rate — the rate you see on Google or XE.com — and charge a small, transparent percentage fee. There are no hidden margins built into the exchange rate.

Wise accepts payments from Saudi bank transfers and Mada/Visa debit cards. Supported Pakistani banks include HBL, MCB, Meezan, UBL, Bank Alfalah, Habib Bank, and all major Pakistani banks with IBAN numbers.

Al Rajhi Bank — Most Trusted Saudi Brand

Al Rajhi Bank has operated in Saudi Arabia since 1957 and is the most recognisable name for Pakistani expats sending money home. While the exchange rate is typically 2–3% below mid-market, the reliability, Arabic-language support, and widespread branch network make it the default choice for many. Best for large transfers where trust matters more than the rate margin.

STC Pay — Fastest Transfer

STC Pay is available to STC mobile subscribers and delivers SAR to PKR in minutes through the STC Pay app. Rates are competitive — often better than Al Rajhi but slightly below Wise. No branch visit required. Particularly popular among younger Pakistani expats already using STC mobile.

5 Tips to Maximise Your SAR to PKR Transfer

  • Compare on the transfer day — PKR fluctuates daily. What's best today may not be best tomorrow.
  • Avoid Fridays and weekends — banks and exchange companies often widen their spreads by 0.5–1% on low-volume days.
  • Choose bank-to-bank — transferring directly to a Pakistani bank account almost always gets a better rate than cash pickup.
  • Use new-user promotions — Remitly and Wise regularly offer zero-fee or boosted-rate first transfers for new accounts.
  • Consolidate transfers — sending SAR 3,000 once is cheaper than SAR 1,000 three times due to flat fees.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best way to send money from Saudi Arabia to Pakistan 2026?+
Wise offers the best SAR to PKR exchange rate (mid-market rate) with low transparent fees. STC Pay is best for speed. Al Rajhi Bank is most trusted for reliability and cash pickup. For first-time transfers, Remitly often has zero-fee promotional offers.
How much can I send from Saudi Arabia to Pakistan?+
There is no official limit on personal remittances for legal residents of Saudi Arabia. Individual service limits apply — Wise: ~SAR 50,000/day; Al Rajhi: up to SAR 100,000/transfer via app. For very large amounts, use your Saudi bank's international wire transfer service.
How long does an SAR to PKR transfer take?+
Wise: 1–2 business days. STC Pay: minutes. Al Rajhi Bank: 1–3 business days. Western Union: minutes (cash pickup). Remitly: minutes to 1 day depending on payment method.
Is Wise available in Saudi Arabia?+
Yes. Wise is fully available to Saudi Arabia residents. You can register using your Iqama and passport. Pay via Saudi bank transfer or Mada/Visa debit card.
