📖 How to Use This End of Service Calculator

Our free End of Service (EOS) Calculator helps employees in Saudi Arabia accurately calculate their gratuity benefits for 2026. Whether you're resigning, being terminated, or retiring, this tool uses official Saudi Labor Law rates to determine your exact EOS amount in Saudi Riyals.

Quick Answer: The Article 84 award is a half-month wage per year for the first 5 years plus a full-month wage per year after that. For resignation, Article 85 pays 1/3 of that award (2–5 years), 2/3 (5–10 years), or the full amount (10+ years). Termination by the employer pays the full award regardless of length of service.

1Enter Your Last Salary

Input your last drawn monthly salary in Saudi Riyals. This should include:

  • Basic salary (mandatory component)
  • Fixed allowances (housing, transport if contractual)
  • Excluded: Overtime, bonuses, commissions (unless contractually guaranteed)

Tip: Check your employment contract to confirm which allowances are considered "fixed" for EOS calculation.

2Enter Years of Service

Calculate your total continuous service with the employer:

  • Count from your official start date to last working day
  • Include probation period if completed
  • Exclude unpaid leave periods
  • Use decimals for partial years (e.g., 7.5 for 7 years 6 months)

3Select Reason for Leaving

This critically affects your EOS calculation:

  • Resignation: Reduced benefits based on years served
  • Termination (no cause): Full Article 84 award regardless of length of service
  • Termination (with cause): May forfeit EOS benefits
  • Retirement: Full benefits regardless of years

4Review Calculation & Legal Rights

The calculator shows:

  • Years of service: All years count toward the Article 84 award
  • Applicable factor: 1/3, 2/3, or full award (per Article 85 on resignation)
  • Total benefit: Your exact EOS amount in SAR
  • Payment timeline: When you should receive payment

5Claim Your Benefits

Per Saudi Labor Law Article 84:

  • Employer must pay EOS within 10 days of termination
  • Payment via bank transfer to your registered account
  • Request written settlement statement
  • Contact HRSD if payment is delayed

Key Legal Provisions:

📜 Article 84: Calculation Basis

EOS calculated on last drawn salary including basic pay and fixed allowances. Variable components excluded unless contractually guaranteed.

⏱️ Article 85: Payment Timeline

Employer must pay EOS benefits within 10 days of employment termination. Delay incurs penalties and interest.

🌍 Article 86: Expatriate Rights

Expatriates have same EOS rights as Saudi nationals. Additional entitlements: return ticket, 30-day notice pay if terminated.

Important: EOS is calculated on continuous service. Breaks in employment (unless approved leave) reset the service clock.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About End of Service

How is End of Service calculated in Saudi Arabia?
EOS is based on years of service and your last wage. The Article 84 base award is a half-month wage for each of the first 5 years plus a full-month wage for each year beyond 5. On resignation, Article 85 reduces this base award to one-third for 2–5 years of service, two-thirds for more than 5 but under 10 years, and the full amount at 10+ years. Termination by the employer pays the full award regardless of length of service.
What salary components are included in EOS calculation?
EOS is calculated on basic salary plus fixed allowances (housing, transport if contractual). Variable allowances like bonuses, overtime, commissions are typically excluded unless specified in employment contract.
Do expatriates get End of Service benefits in Saudi Arabia?
Yes, expatriates are entitled to End of Service benefits under Saudi Labor Law if they complete at least 2 years of continuous service. The calculation follows the same formula as Saudi nationals.
What's the difference between resignation and termination for EOS?
If you resign, the Article 84 base award is reduced under Article 85 (one-third for 2–5 years, two-thirds for 5–10 years, full at 10+ years). If the employer terminates without cause, you receive the full Article 84 award regardless of length of service. Termination with cause may forfeit benefits.
When is End of Service paid?
EOS benefits must be paid within 10 days of employment termination per Saudi Labor Law. Payment is typically made via bank transfer to your registered account.
Can I receive EOS in installments?
EOS is typically paid as a lump sum. However, mutual agreement between employee and employer can allow installment payments, though this is not common practice.
Is EOS taxable in Saudi Arabia?
End of Service benefits are generally tax-free for employees in Saudi Arabia. However, consult a tax advisor for your specific situation, especially if you have other income sources.
What if I change jobs before 2 years?
If you leave before completing 2 years of continuous service, you are not entitled to End of Service benefits under Saudi Labor Law. However, you still receive any unpaid salary and accrued vacation pay.
