How to Calculate Zakat on Gold (2026 Guide)

Calculating Zakat is a spiritual and financial duty. This guide explains exactly how to do it for your gold assets held in Saudi Arabia or Pakistan.

Step 1: Check the Nisab

The Nisab for gold is 87.48 grams (or 7.5 Tolas). If your total gold weight is less than this, you do not owe Zakat.

Step 2: Determine Total Weight

Include all your gold: bars, coins, and jewelry. Note: In some schools of thought, jewelry worn for daily use is exempt, but many prefer to pay Zakat on all gold to be safe.

Step 3: Find Current Market Value

Use the current market price of gold (e.g., SAR 564.26 for 24K). Multiply your weight by this price to get the total value.

Step 4: Calculate 2.5%

The Zakat rate is fixed at 2.5%. Divide your total value by 40 to find the exact amount you need to pay.

Example: If you own 100g of 24K gold valued at 56,426 SAR, your Zakat is 1,410.65 SAR.
