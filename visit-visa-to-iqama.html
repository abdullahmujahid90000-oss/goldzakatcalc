🔄 Saudi Visit Visa → Iqama Conversion Guide 2026

Who can convert a visit visa to a family Iqama — and who cannot. Children under 18: ✅ YES. Wife/Spouse: ❌ NO. Here's exactly what to do in each case.

📅 Updated May 2026 🏛️ Jawazat Process 💰 SAR 2,000 per child ✅ Based on real experience

⚡ The Direct Answer — Can You Convert?

👶
✅ YES — CAN CONVERT

Children Under 18

Children currently in Saudi Arabia on a visit visa can convert to a family Iqama at Jawazat, without leaving the country. Fee: SAR 2,000 per child + SAR 200 Iqama printing.

👩
❌ NO — CANNOT CONVERT

Wife / Spouse

A wife on a visit visa cannot convert to an Iqama inside Saudi Arabia. She must exit the country. The sponsor then applies for a family residence visa — she enters on the correct visa type.

🚨 IMPORTANT — Read this before you try anything: Many expats are told incorrectly that the wife can convert. She cannot. Jawazat will not process the conversion for a spouse, only for children under 18. Attempting to "find a way" through middlemen (agents) is illegal and results in fines + deportation. The only correct path for a wife is to exit and re-enter on the correct visa.

👶 Converting a Child's Visit Visa to Iqama — Step by Step

Prerequisites: Child must currently be in Saudi Arabia on a valid visit visa. Father's Iqama must be valid. Attested birth certificate is mandatory. Medical test/biometric enrollment is required first.
1
MEDICAL

Complete Medical Test & Biometric Enrollment

Take the child to an approved medical centre for the residency medical test. This includes blood test, chest X-ray (for children above a certain age — doctor determines) and biometric fingerprinting. You will receive a medical report certificate (usually same day or next day).

Book at approved centres via Seha app (صحة) or walk in to major medical centres. Cost: SAR 200–400 per child.
2
ABSHER

Book Jawazat Appointment via Absher

Log in to Absher → Individuals → Services → Passports → Book Appointment → Select your nearest Jawazat (Directorate of Passports) office → Choose "Family Visa Conversion" or "Residence Permit" service → Book a slot.

absher.sa→Individuals→Services→Passports & Visas→Book Appointment→Jawazat→Family Iqama Conversion
3
DOCUMENTS

Prepare Your Documents

Gather ALL documents listed below before your appointment. Missing even one document means you will be turned away and must rebook.

4
JAWAZAT

Attend Jawazat Office

Father (Iqama sponsor) must attend in person with the child. Present all documents. The officer will verify the child's visit visa, father's Iqama, and medical clearance. Fingerprints are taken again at the counter.

⚠️ Both the father AND the child should attend. Some offices accept the father alone with child's passport — but to avoid issues, bring the child. Confirm your specific office's policy when booking.
5
SADAD

Pay the Fee

SAR 2,000 family visa fee per child + SAR 200 Iqama card printing fee = SAR 2,200 per child. Pay via SADAD bill payment, Al Rajhi, SNB, or other Saudi bank. You will be given a fee reference number at the counter.

6
COLLECT

Collect Iqama Card

The Iqama card is usually ready within 5–10 working days. You can track via Absher. The Iqama is mailed to the address registered on your sponsor profile, or you can collect from the Jawazat office. The Iqama is valid for 1 year and must be renewed annually.

📄 Documents Required — Child Iqama Conversion

  • Father's valid Iqama (original + copy)
  • Father's valid passport (copy — bio page)
  • Child's valid passport (original + copy — must have valid visit visa stamp)
  • Child's attested birth certificate — certified by home country + authenticated by Saudi embassy before arrival
  • Medical test clearance certificate (from step 1)
  • Health insurance policy — valid for minimum 1 year
  • 4 passport-size photos of the child (white background)
  • ⚠️ If child was born in Saudi Arabia (e.g. twins): hospital birth report + civil registration certificate from the municipality (أمانة) is also needed in addition to the birth certificate
  • ⚠️ All foreign documents must be attested (notarised in home country + authenticated by Saudi MOFA or Saudi embassy abroad). Documents not attested will be rejected.

👩 Wife's Visit Visa Expired / About to Expire — What To Do

Since a wife cannot convert her visit visa to an Iqama, you have these options depending on your situation:

Option A — Extend the Visit Visa (Short-Term)

If the visit visa has not yet expired, extend it via Absher before the deadline. Extensions are 30–60 days each. You can get 1–2 extensions typically. Good if you're still preparing documents for the residence Iqama.

Option B — Wife Exits, You Apply for Residence Visa (Best Option)

Wife exits Saudi Arabia. You apply via Istiqdam office for a family residence visa (the proper Iqama path). Once approved, wife enters Saudi Arabia on the correct entry permit and Iqama is issued. This is the standard permanent solution. → See full Istiqdam guide

Option C — Wife Exits, Re-enters on New Visit Visa (Repeat Pattern)

If you're not yet ready for permanent Iqama (e.g. missing documents), wife can exit and apply for a new visit visa. This is legal but creates an ongoing cost (SAR 300 + insurance per visa). Not ideal long-term but buys time.

❌ Option D — Do Nothing (VERY BAD)

If the visit visa expires and wife stays without renewal or exit, she becomes an illegal resident. Fines start at SAR 500 and can reach SAR 5,000+ with deportation and multi-year entry ban. This must be avoided at all costs.

Extend visit visa via Absher:
absher.sa→Individuals→Services→Visa Services→Extend Visit Visa→Select spouse→Pay extension fee (SAR 100–300)

👶 Special Case: Children Born in Saudi Arabia

If your child was born inside Saudi Arabia (e.g. your wife was on a visit visa when she gave birth), the process is slightly different:

  • Get the hospital birth report (تقرير ولادة) immediately — do not delay
  • Register the birth at the Civil Affairs office (أحوال مدنية) using the hospital report + father's Iqama + mother's documents
  • Obtain the Saudi Civil Registration Birth Certificate — this is your primary document for Iqama application
  • A newborn does not automatically receive a visit visa — the Iqama application must be started within 90 days of birth to avoid fines
  • After 90 days, the child is technically overstaying — fines apply immediately
💬 Important Note on the 90-Day Rule:

A child born in Saudi Arabia does not enter on a visit visa — they have a short window (effectively the first 90 days) where they're treated as a new resident under the father's sponsorship. After 90 days, you must have the Iqama in process. Start the Jawazat paperwork as soon as you have the birth certificate — don't wait.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can my wife convert her visit visa to an Iqama without leaving Saudi Arabia?
No. This is a firm rule. A wife (spouse) cannot convert a visit visa to an Iqama inside Saudi Arabia. The correct process is: she exits Saudi Arabia, you apply for a family residence visa through Istiqdam, she enters on the new visa, and the Iqama is issued. See our Istiqdam guide for the full process.
How long does the child Iqama conversion take at Jawazat?
The actual appointment at Jawazat is usually 30–60 minutes if you have all documents. The Iqama card is then printed and ready within 5–10 working days. You will receive an SMS when it's ready.
My child's visit visa expired — can I still convert at Jawazat?
Technically you need a valid visit visa to convert. If the visit visa has expired, an overstay fine applies first — you must pay it via SADAD before Jawazat will process the conversion. Fines: SAR 500 for 1–30 days overstay. Pay early, then proceed with conversion.
Can I start the family residence visa process while my wife is still on a visit visa in Saudi Arabia?
Yes — you can start the Istiqdam paperwork while she is still on her visit visa. This is smart planning. But the actual family residence Iqama issuance and entry permit (تصريح الدخول) is issued for re-entry — she still needs to exit and re-enter on the new visa before the Iqama is stamped.
Does the SAR 2,000 fee apply per child or per family?
Per child. There is no family discount. If you have twins, you pay SAR 2,000 × 2 = SAR 4,000. If you have three children converting, SAR 6,000 total. Each child's Iqama is issued individually.
My child was born in Saudi Arabia on a visit visa — what do I do?
Get the hospital birth report, register at Civil Affairs for the birth certificate, then start the Iqama application at Jawazat. Do this within 90 days of birth. The process is the same as visit visa conversion but uses the Saudi birth certificate instead of a foreign birth certificate.
